# Army (2025)
**Source:** [Victoria 3 Military Tutorial Part One - Army in 2025](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lbVIrfqjP7Q) (51:22 runtime)

## Summary
Victoria 3 is an economy-centred game, so the Army is first and foremost a supply-chain problem: every Battalion type, mobilization option, and Production Method consumes specific goods, and the farther/larger your force the heavier the bill. This note covers Battalions and Barracks, Conscription Centers, Generals, the four Army Model laws, mobilization options, army composition, and the limited tactical levers (orders, strategic objectives, naval invasions, multi-front pressure).

## Core mechanics
1. **Battalion upgrades are one-way** — Each Battalion is Infantry, Cavalry, or Artillery, and upgrading to a higher unit type (e.g. Skirmish Infantry) permanently changes its input goods. DECISION RULE: before upgrading, check the new input goods against your Market — if you cannot afford the supply burden, leave it unupgraded; the only way back is to disband and rebuild. `[03:01]`
2. **Veterancy grows with age** — Battalions accumulate veterancy bonuses to offence/defence over time. DECISION RULE: when disbanding to cut costs, always delete the newest / lowest-veterancy Battalions first. `[04:01]`
3. **Manpower vs Battalion count** — Manpower is the actual pop count in an Army; two armies with the same Battalion count can have wildly different Manpower after casualties. DECISION RULE: when deciding whether to engage, compare offence/defence AND Manpower — never Battalion count alone. `[05:32]`
4. **General organization cap** — Each General organizes 30 Battalions at base rank; exceed the cap and the Army loses organization. DECISION RULE: if you need more capacity, promote (half the bureaucracy cost of hiring) when bureaucracy is tight; hire when you also want to shape Interest Group power. `[08:32]`
5. **Barracks officers require Primary Culture pops** — Officers come from middle/upper strata and need full (level 5) cultural acceptance, which in practice means Primary Culture. DECISION RULE: do not build Battalions in states with few or no Primary Culture pops, or accept the lower-tier Production Method (No Organized Training) and a worse training rate. `[14:31]`
6. **Army Model law shapes the standing/conscript mix** — The law sets Barracks max level per state, conscription cap per state, conscription rate, and total conscript Battalions. DECISION RULE: low-population economies should lean conscript (National Militia / Mass Conscription / Peasant Levies) so pops stay in industry; pop-rich nations can afford Professional Army. `[16:30]`
7. **Mobilization options are good costs that only bite on mobilization** — Supplies, supplements, transportation, and medical add Battalion bonuses and add their input goods to your Barracks ONLY after mobilization (and add 60% to overall Army goods cost). DECISION RULE: before mobilizing, audit your Market for every selected good (especially supplement crops) and add trade routes / shift PMs if a spike would break supply. `[27:02]`
8. **Pick the cheapest supplement available** — Tobacco, Liquor, and Opium consume 0.5 units/Battalion; Chocolate consumes 1.0 sugar/Battalion. DECISION RULE: prefer Tobacco/Liquor/Opium over Chocolate whenever they exist in your Market. `[28:30]`
9. **First Aid for Recovery Rate** — Injured soldiers either die, become dependents (lost to workforce), or return to the line at a speed governed by Recovery Rate. DECISION RULE: unlock and enable First Aid as soon as available — high Recovery Rate keeps Manpower up mid-war. `[30:31]`
10. **Conscripts are pulled from civilian jobs** — Adding conscripts in a state spawns a Conscription Center which hires those pops away from buildings, but only after mobilization. DECISION RULE: always keep a real standing Army — conscripts are too slow to be the only force; treat them as reinforcements layered onto regulars. `[33:01]`
11. **Cavalry + Artillery ≤ Infantry** — If combined Cavalry and Artillery Battalions exceed Infantry, the Army takes an organization penalty. DECISION RULE: keep the inequality satisfied in every Army, recounting whenever you add/upgrade units. `[36:01]`
12. **Multi-Army doctrine (Main / Cavalry / National Guard / Landing)** — Specialize Armies: Main = infantry+artillery with one offensive + one defensive General; Cavalry = infantry+cavalry, all on offensive orders (e.g. Rapid Advance) for breakthroughs; National Guard = infantry (or +artillery), defensive orders only, ideal home for conscripts; Landing = mixed infantry+cavalry for Naval Invasions, no conscripts. DECISION RULE: assign each Army a single role and lock its General orders to match — do not mix offence and defence inside one Army. `[35:31]`
13. **General orders are conditional bonuses** — Advance Front boosts advancement speed + offence; Defend Front boosts defence; specialty orders (e.g. Rapid Advance requires ≥30% Cavalry) need composition or trait prerequisites. DECISION RULE: build the Army composition to unlock the order you want, not the other way round. `[37:00]`
14. **Strategic objectives steer pathfinding** — Setting a state as Strategic Objective in the military lens makes Generals push toward it. DECISION RULE: use objectives to direct landed Armies toward link-ups with other fronts instead of nibbling adjacent provinces. `[46:31]`
15. **Manufacture extra fronts** — A war begins with the fronts your borders give you, but Naval Invasions create new fronts the enemy must split forces to defend. DECISION RULE: against a larger-Manpower opponent, plan at least one Naval Invasion of an Incorporated State to halve the effective force-ratio on the main front. `[45:31]`
16. **Naval Invasion sizing** — One Army + one Navy per invasion; Naval Invasion efficiency drops below 100% if Battalions > Flotillas. DECISION RULE: size the Landing Army to the chosen Navy's Flotilla count and never exceed it. `[41:30]`
17. **Naval Invasion can be lost** — Enemy Navy can intercept (lose at sea = invasion ends); on land, losing three battles ends the invasion. DECISION RULE: only invade with naval superiority or against a country with no Navy in range; keep a reinforcement plan if the landing front opens. `[50:03]`

## Game numbers & rules of thumb
- Skirmish Infantry consumes 1 Ammunition + 2 Small Arms per Battalion (input goods scale per-Battalion). `[03:01]`
- Mobilizing an Army increases its goods consumption by ~60%. `[07:00]`
- Base General organization cap = 30 Battalions; promotion costs half the bureaucracy of hiring. `[08:32]` `[10:30]`
- Army Model Barracks max-level per state: Peasant Levies 25, Professional Army 100, National Militia 5, Mass Conscription 100. `[18:00]` `[18:30]`
- Army Model conscription bonuses: Peasant Levies +25 cap / +4% rate, Professional Army +50 / +1%, National Militia +100 / +5%, Mass Conscription +100 / +3%. `[19:30]`
- Total conscript Battalions (sample US figures): Peasant Levies 220, Professional Army 46, National Militia 289, Mass Conscription high. `[21:31]`
- Peasant Levies: −10% military goods cost (buyer-side only; producers still receive full price). `[17:00]`
- Peasant Levies: −25% weekly unit experience gain — fine to start with, bad to stay on. `[22:31]`
- Professional Army: +100% weekly unit experience gain; Mass Conscription: +100% training rate. `[23:31]` `[24:31]`
- Basic Supplies: +25% to current input goods + 0.5 Grain per Battalion. `[27:32]`
- Supplements: Tobacco/Liquor/Opium = 0.5 unit/Battalion; Chocolate = 1.0 Sugar/Battalion. `[28:30]`
- Forced March: free at start, costs morale, gives formation speed + mobilization speed. `[29:31]`
- Naval Invasion: 1 Army + 1 Navy; efficiency = 100% only while Battalions ≤ Flotillas; 3 lost land battles = invasion fails. `[41:30]` `[50:03]`

## Common pitfalls
- Upgrading Battalions you cannot supply — there is no downgrade button; you must disband and rebuild from scratch. `[03:31]`
- Building Barracks in states without Primary Culture pops, then wondering why officers will not hire. `[14:31]`
- Treating mobilization input goods as free — Chocolate on 50 mobilized Battalions = 50 extra Sugar overnight; check the Market first. `[32:00]`
- Forgetting that conscripts leave civilian jobs when mobilized — over-mobilizing wrecks your economy mid-war. `[34:32]`
- Mobilizing conscripts past an Army's General organization cap and quietly losing organization. `[35:02]`
- Stuffing all Battalions onto the single starting front against a higher-Manpower enemy instead of opening a second front via Naval Invasion. `[46:01]`
- Letting a Landing Army sit idle after invasions are done — always reassign it to a front. `[41:03]`
- Naval-invading with more Battalions than Flotillas, taking an efficiency penalty for no reason. `[41:30]`

## Cheatsheet
1. Pick an Army Model that matches your pop budget (low pops → conscript-heavy laws; high pops → Professional Army); enable National Guard for extra conscription room.
2. Build Barracks only in Primary-Culture states; run General Training PM; upgrade Battalion types only after confirming Market supply.
3. Organize four Armies: Main (Inf+Arty, 1 offence + 1 defence General), Cavalry (Inf+Cav, Rapid Advance), National Guard (Inf, defensive, conscripts live here), Landing (Inf+Cav, sized to Flotillas, no conscripts).
4. Keep Cavalry+Artillery ≤ Infantry in every Army; promote a General before exceeding the 30-Battalion cap.
5. Set mobilization options: Basic/Extra Supplies + cheapest supplement (Tobacco/Liquor/Opium) + Forced March + First Aid as soon as researched — then audit input goods before mobilizing.
6. In war, open extra fronts via Naval Invasion of weakly-defended Incorporated States and use Strategic Objectives to steer Armies toward link-ups.
