# War: Diplomatic Plays, Fronts, and Naval Invasions

**Source:** [Victoria 3 War Tutorial - Transvaal Naval Invades the British Mainland](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EHpM-uCoAFE) (40:45 runtime)

## Summary
A war's outcome is largely decided during the Diplomatic Play phase — which Wargoals you pick determines how the war can end. Once fighting starts, the player's main levers are mobilization timing, Front manpower balancing, Navy orders, and Naval Invasions. Picking a Wargoal you cannot actually occupy (e.g. War Reparations against a country whose only incorporated states are on a mainland you can't reach) can lock the enemy's war score above zero and force you into a desperate invasion or default.

## Core mechanics

1. **Diplomatic Play maneuvering phase** — When a Play begins, the game is paused while both sides add Wargoals and try to Sway third parties. DECISION RULE: pause and do all military setup here; Sway likely allies before adding Wargoals (added Wargoals raise Infamy, scaring off potential allies). `[00:31]` `[07:30]` `[08:01]`

2. **Wargoal selection determines win conditions** — To push the enemy's war score below zero you must occupy their capital state OR every state tied to each Wargoal you added. DECISION RULE: never add a Wargoal you can't realistically occupy. In particular, War Reparations requires occupying an *incorporated* state of the *target* — not of their subject. `[08:32]` `[25:32]`

3. **Infamy threshold from stacking Wargoals** — Each added Wargoal raises Infamy. Crossing 25 jumps you to the next Infamy tier and turns potential allies against you. DECISION RULE: check whether the marginal Wargoal will push you past 25 before adding it. `[08:01]`

4. **Enlistment Efforts decree** — Adds +5% Conscription Rate and +25% Training Rate to a state. DECISION RULE: apply to high-employment states before war; it scales with workforce size, so populous employed states give the biggest manpower boost. `[04:00]` `[04:31]`

5. **Mobilization Options trade-offs** — Transportation options speed front-to-front movement; Medical Support increases recovery rate (wounded returning to battle). DECISION RULE: skip Transportation on garrison/defensive armies that won't redeploy; always take Medical Support — recovery rate directly preserves manpower. `[05:31]` `[06:01]`

6. **Pre-war army filling** — Conscripts take real time to hire and mobilize. DECISION RULE: at the first pause of the Play, fill every General to max battalions, add extra Generals so no conscripts sit idle, and click Mobilize immediately. Time before unpause is free preparation. `[05:06]` `[06:30]`

7. **Front manpower balancing** — Hover the Front circles to compare total manpower and average power (offense/defense weighted). DECISION RULE: rebalance Generals/armies between Fronts when one side is significantly outnumbered; raw manpower can be offset by quality but not by huge gaps. `[10:03]` `[10:30]` `[19:30]`

8. **General orders — Advance vs Defend** — An army on pure Defend never pushes; it only holds. DECISION RULE: if you start *losing* ground on a defensive Front, switch at least one General to Advance Front to claw it back. `[17:30]` `[18:02]`

9. **Naval commander orders (Intercept / Raid / Escort)** — Intercept engages enemy fleets in the same sea node; Raid sinks enemy Convoys; Escort protects yours. DECISION RULE: stack multiple commanders on one Navy with different orders; watch the red bottom-right notifications for sea nodes where your Convoys are being sunk and dispatch Escort/Intercept there. `[14:00]` `[14:30]` `[15:01]`

10. **Defending against Naval Invasion** — Armies stationed at an HQ with orders set to Defend will automatically engage any Naval Invasion landing in any state of that HQ. DECISION RULE: once you've fully taken a coastal region (no more land Front), park a defensive army at that HQ instead of pushing it all to the remaining Front. `[23:34]` `[24:01]`

11. **Naval Invasion sizing rule** — You may land only as many Battalions as you have Flotillas without taking a penalty; the defender can stack their full HQ garrison. DECISION RULE: scout the target state's defender count first, and pick the weakest-defended coastal state. The defender always has a structural edge, so go where they're thinnest. `[27:33]` `[28:00]`

12. **Naval Invasion as a routing tool to inland HQs** — Coastal HQs may have a dotted "land crossing" line to an adjacent HQ. DECISION RULE: land in the weak coastal HQ, create a Front, then march armies overland into the richer inland HQ (e.g. capital). `[28:00]` `[28:31]`

13. **Navy repair downtime** — A defeated Navy returns to its HQ; all movement options grey out until a "ready" notification fires. DECISION RULE: don't queue an invasion you can't escort — wait for the repair notification before launching the next attempt. `[24:30]` `[25:01]` `[31:30]`

14. **Companies as war prep** — Some Companies grant prosperity bonuses to military goods cost or army offense. DECISION RULE: before declaring or expecting war, establish military-relevant Companies (generic ones like -10% military goods cost and +5% army offense are universally available). `[12:33]` `[13:01]`

15. **Race-to-minus-100 endgame** — When neither side can be pushed below zero normally, the war becomes a sprint: whoever first occupies enough enemy territory (especially the capital) to drive the *other* side to -100 wins. DECISION RULE: once you've started occupying their capital, abandon offensive frontline pushes and consolidate to defend *your* capital — both clocks run simultaneously. `[37:32]` `[38:01]`

## Game numbers & rules of thumb
- Enlistment Efforts decree: +5% Conscription Rate, +25% Training Rate per state `[04:31]`
- Infamy tier change at 25 — crossing it makes other countries refuse to join your side `[08:01]`
- Default penalty: -5% army offense and defense while at debt limit; also pauses your construction queue `[31:01]` `[33:05]`
- Naval Invasion: max Battalions landed = number of Flotillas in the Navy without penalty `[28:00]`
- Naval commander orders: Intercept / Raid / Escort — you can stack multiple commanders per Navy `[14:30]`
- War Reparations Wargoal requires occupying an *incorporated* state of the target country (or their capital) to drive war score below zero `[26:00]`
- Best preparation window for an army is from Play start to first unpause — use it fully `[06:30]`

## Common pitfalls
- **Adding War Reparations against a country whose incorporated states are unreachable.** Subjects' incorporated states do NOT count — only the *target* nation's. You will be unable to push war score below zero. `[08:32]` `[25:32]`
- **Sway attempts after raising Infamy.** Add Wargoals only after you've finished swaying allies; passing Infamy 25 mid-Play can flip a likely ally into a refusal. `[08:01]`
- **Unexpected third Fronts from enemy subjects.** A small subject's territory bordering you opens an extra attack vector. Scan the political map for subject borders before declaring. `[11:34]` `[12:01]`
- **Leaving a coastal HQ undefended after clearing its land Front.** The enemy will Naval Invade what was previously safe; assign a defensive army to the HQ. `[23:34]`
- **Naval Invading with more Battalions than you have Flotillas.** Triggers the invasion penalty; either shrink the landing army or wait for shipyard capacity. `[29:30]`
- **Letting a war drag on against a richer opponent.** They can simply outlast you to default; once defaulted you lose 5% offense/defense and construction stops — a doom spiral. `[31:01]` `[33:30]`
- **Pulling every army onto the offensive Front near the end.** If you also leave your own capital Front thin, both war scores race to -100 and you can lose first. `[37:32]`

## Cheatsheet
1. Diplomatic Play pause: Sway allies FIRST, then add only Wargoals you can actually occupy; keep Infamy under 25 if allies matter.
2. Apply Enlistment Efforts to your highest-employment states; fill every General to max Battalions; add Medical Support mobilization; click Mobilize.
3. Assign offensive armies (main + cavalry) to the Front with your Wargoals; defensive National Guard to holding Fronts; rebalance by hovering Front circles.
4. Run Navies with stacked commanders (Intercept + Raid + Escort); watch the red Convoy-sunk alerts and dispatch Escorts.
5. After taking a coastal region, leave a Defend-order army at its HQ to absorb counter-invasions; only then push remaining troops to the last Front.
6. For Naval Invasion: pick the weakest-defended coastal state, match Battalions to Flotillas, use dotted land-crossings to march inland toward their capital.
