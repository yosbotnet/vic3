# Deficit Spending

**Source:** [Deficit Spending Tutorial in Victoria 3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vNH7pYJ_3JI) (42:25 runtime)

## Summary
Deficit spending is a mid-to-late-game concept (not a discrete mechanic) where you borrow against your buildings' cash reserves to fuel a self-reinforcing loop: more construction creates more profitable buildings, which raise both your debt limit and your minting income, which in turn fund still more construction. The video explains the loop, the laws and country conditions that make it safe, and walks through an example with the United States from 1873 onward, growing GDP from 111 million to 310 million pounds in ten years. Use this knowledge once you are at least a major power with a stable economy and want to go for high-GDP or billionaire-achievement runs.

## Core mechanics
1. **Debt and the debt limit** — Your debt limit is the sum of the cash reserves of all your buildings. Borrowing pulls money from those reserves, and interest payments flow back to the building owners (pops like Dixie Protestant capitalists), who then spend it in your market. Debt itself is harmless; only hitting the debt limit (default) is bad.
2. **The deficit spending loop** — Construction creates buildings, profitable buildings increase cash reserves (raising your debt limit) and add minting income (lowering weekly expenses), which enables more construction. As long as your debt limit grows faster than your debt, you can run the loop indefinitely.
3. **Construction Sector priority** — Construction goods are your biggest weekly expense, so government construction queues should be dominated by Construction Sectors and the input goods (paper, coal, tools, glass, steel, explosives) that keep their cost down.
4. **Laissez-Faire economic system** — The private sector takes 75% of construction points (and pays for 75% of construction goods), and the law also gives -25% loan interest rate. Interventionism is possible but laissez-faire is strongly preferred.
5. **No Migration Controls + Multiculturalism** — You need a constant stream of mass migrations to staff the buildings you keep adding. Cultural Exclusion technically works, but Multiculturalism yields more migration.
6. **Graduated Taxation** — Introduces a 20% dividend tax (vs. 10% under Proportional). Since most of your economy is privately owned and capitalists pocket your interest payments, dividend taxes scale up nicely with the loop.
7. **Free Trade (or Mercantilism)** — You will produce huge surpluses, so trade-advantage bonuses on exports matter. Free Trade also keeps industrialists happy, who become powerful as you industrialise.
8. **Interest rate management** — Interest rate is tied to country rank (need major or great power), and is reduced by Laissez-Faire, certain techs, treasury bonds, and a happy/powerful petite bourgeoisie.
9. **GDP, production methods, and minting** — GDP feeds minting, which is direct government income. New PMs cause sharp GDP dips because input goods are subtracted from GDP, but each PM unlock raises productivity past the previous peak.
10. **Pause-all safety valve** — If your weekly balance turns red while paused (visible by colour on the budget number), the government can't afford to keep building. Pause government construction to let the private sector and investment pool carry the load, then unpause when debt utilisation drops back below 50%.
11. **Investment pool health** — Pool balance matters less than its weekly inflow. If the inflow stalls, either your construction goods are too expensive or regional HQs of foreign companies are siphoning reinvestment back to their home country.
12. **Regional HQs (yours and theirs)** — Foreign regional HQs in your country employ your pops but send reinvestment home, capping your effective growth. The countermeasure is building your own regional HQs abroad to siphon reinvestment back.

## Common pitfalls
- Hitting the debt limit triggers default and bankruptcy — the only thing that actually makes debt dangerous.
- Going to war while deficit spending: mobilised battalions cost +60% goods, which can break the loop. White-peace out of opportunistic wars.
- Letting construction-good prices spiral: drains the investment pool and pushes weekly balance red.
- Ignoring foreign regional HQs in your country — they employ your pops but pull reinvestment out.
- Keeping pops as laborers: low wages mean less consumption; use PMs (e.g. threshing machines) to push them into better-paying jobs.
- Hoarding investment-pool balance is unnecessary; what matters is keeping its weekly inflow positive.
- Subsidies left over from wars (e.g. groceries) bleed the budget once the war ends — turn them off.
- Bureaucracy deficits silently waste tax revenue; build government admin (ideally where the White House buffs them) before scaling further.

## Cheatsheet
1. Get to major or great power, then pass Laissez-Faire, No Migration Controls, Multiculturalism, Free Trade, and work toward Graduated Taxation.
2. Keep the petite bourgeoisie happy and powerful (treasury bonds, friendly head of IG) to slash interest rate.
3. Fill your government queue with Construction Sectors plus their input goods (paper, coal, tools, glass, steel, explosives) — nothing else.
4. Monitor debt utilisation: under 50% keep building; above 50% pause-all and let the private sector run until it falls again.
5. Watch the investment pool's weekly inflow (not balance); if it sags, fix construction-good prices and consider regional HQs abroad.
6. Avoid wars (+60% mobilised goods cost); white-peace out and stay focused on the loop.
