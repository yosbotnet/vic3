# Foreign Investment Rights

**Source:** [Foreign Investment Rights Tutorial in Victoria 3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_kXcatlJMkU) (27:25 runtime)

## Summary
Foreign Investment lets one country build buildings inside another. It can be granted via a Treaty article or via the Foreign Investment 3 mandate in a Power Bloc (works on lower-ranked members). Granting rights speeds GDP growth but risks draining your Investment Pool through foreign Regional HQs; receiving rights is generally good, but should be paired with eventual nationalization of foreign-owned buildings.

## Core mechanics
1. **Granting investment rights (Treaty)** — A Treaty article that flows one direction per article: either you get rights in them, they get rights in you, or both. DECISION RULE: only grant rights to a partner if you also secure rights back, so your Investment Pool has a counter-income stream `[00:30]` `[20:31]`.
2. **Foreign Investment 3 Power Bloc mandate** — Gives you Foreign Investment rights in any bloc member of *lower Rank* than you. DECISION RULE: as a Great Power, lead a bloc and take this mandate to gain free build access in all junior members `[01:00]` `[13:30]`.
3. **Private-sector foreign builds (no charter)** — With investment rights, your Financial Districts / Manor Houses / Company main HQs can build abroad; those building levels are owned by the *main HQ at home*, so dividends flow to your pops `[01:30]` `[04:02]`.
4. **Investment Rights Charter (Company)** — A Company charter that allows that Company to establish Regional Headquarters in foreign countries. DECISION RULE: grant it to Companies you want extracting wealth abroad; without it they can still build, but only via the main HQ `[03:31]` `[04:02]`.
5. **Regional HQ creation** — Triggers automatically once a Company's main HQ owns >=5 building levels in a foreign country (and has the Charter). You can't manually place one `[04:31]` `[07:30]`.
6. **Regional HQ economics (when YOU own it abroad)** — Buildings under the Regional HQ are owned by *foreign pops*, who get the dividends and pay tax to *their* country, BUT the *reinvestment* flows back to *your* Investment Pool; new levels are built using the host country's Investment Pool and private construction queue. DECISION RULE: prioritise Regional HQs in large, resource-rich, populous nations (USA, Qing, France, Germany) — small/under-developed hosts can't build much `[05:31]` `[06:00]` `[21:01]`.
7. **Regional HQ damage (when foreigners own one in YOU)** — Reinvestment leaves your country instead of feeding your Investment Pool. DECISION RULE: watch the weekly Investment Pool change — if it stays red, nationalize foreign building levels until the bleed stops `[14:32]` `[26:00]`.
8. **Nationalizing foreign-owned levels** — Open a building, click Nationalize, select only the levels owned by the foreign Regional HQ (the game appears to remove Regional HQ levels first when partially nationalizing). The Regional HQ disappears once it owns 0 levels. DECISION RULE: under Laissez-Faire you cannot nationalize at all — switch laws, or take the buildings via war `[08:31]` `[10:31]` `[11:00]`.
9. **Free nationalization** — Command Economy + Cooperative Ownership lets you nationalize without paying. DECISION RULE: if you need to expel many Regional HQs, switch to these laws first to avoid debt `[09:30]`.
10. **Debt-spiral chain** — Negative Investment Pool change → pool drains → private queue stops → government pays 100% of construction → budget collapses. DECISION RULE: treat a sustained-negative Investment Pool as a fire; if you can't nationalize, lower construction cost (cheaper inputs, more domestic input production) `[15:30]` `[26:31]`.
11. **GDP / minting interaction** — Goods produced inside a host country count toward *that country's* GDP and minting, even if owned by you. DECISION RULE: hosts benefit too — granting rights is not purely a sacrifice; use it as a diplomatic carrot `[07:00]`.
12. **Prestige goods abroad** — Buildings owned by your Company in a foreign country can produce that Company's prestige good *in the host's market*. Useful for transferring goods access without trade `[07:00]`.

## Game numbers & rules of thumb
- Foreign Investment 3 mandate: applies to bloc members of *lower Rank* than the holder `[01:00]`.
- Regional HQ threshold: Company main HQ must own **5+ building levels** in the foreign country `[04:31]`.
- Reinvestment from a foreign-owned Regional HQ leaves *your* country and goes to *their* Investment Pool `[05:31]`.
- Under Laissez-Faire: private sector covers 75% of construction points and cost; if private queue halts, government takes the full bill `[15:30]`.
- Laissez-Faire forbids nationalization entirely `[11:00]`.
- Command Economy + Cooperative Ownership = free nationalization `[09:30]`.
- An over-large Investment Pool is wasted money — large idle stacks are bad in general `[18:30]`.
- Possible bug (as of 1.9): reported reinvestment from foreign Regional HQs into your pool can be several times the sum of individual HQ contributions; if patched, Regional HQs become much less lucrative `[23:31]` `[25:01]`.
- A persistently negative weekly Investment Pool change is the trigger threshold for action; brief dips into red are tolerable `[26:00]`.

## Common pitfalls
- Giving rights away without securing reciprocal rights — your Investment Pool only loses income, never gains `[20:31]`.
- Being on Laissez-Faire when you suddenly need to expel a foreign Regional HQ — you can't nationalize until you change laws or win a war `[11:00]`.
- Ignoring the Investment Pool weekly change number until the pool is nearly empty — by then you're already in a debt spiral and nationalization costs will deepen the hole `[17:00]` `[18:00]`.
- Granting rights to weak hosts (low pop, low infrastructure) — they won't build much, so you gain little while still suffering the outbound reinvestment when they reciprocate `[21:30]`.
- Treating granted investment rights as permanent — the tutorial's stance is they should always end with you nationalizing foreign-owned buildings `[13:01]`.

## Cheatsheet
1. Lead a Power Bloc as a Great Power and take Foreign Investment 3 — free build access in lower-ranked members.
2. Grant the Investment Rights Charter to a strong Company, get rights in a populous nation; let them hit 5 levels to spawn a Regional HQ.
3. If granting rights *to* others, always negotiate rights *from* them in the same Treaty.
4. Watch the Investment Pool weekly change every few years; sustained red = act now.
5. To remove a foreign Regional HQ: open each owned building, Nationalize only its levels (not the main HQ's); switch off Laissez-Faire first.
6. Plan to eventually nationalize all foreign-owned buildings in your country — treat granted rights as a temporary growth boost, not a permanent arrangement.
