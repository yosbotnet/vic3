# Playing as a Small Nation (2025)
**Source:** [How to Play as a Small Nation in Victoria 3 in 2025](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5lFVLfd1w3o) (40:55 runtime)

## Summary
Small nations invert several "standard" Vic3 strategies because their tiny Treasury, low Rank, and brutal interest rate punish the debt-funded expansion that great powers rely on. The video walks three openings (Congo, Herat, Mascara) to show how to read your starting military threat, build economic inputs *before* a Construction Sector, exploit the Investment Pool to stay solvent, and use Peasant Levies plus universities to catch up. The throughline: protect the Treasury, deter aggression rather than match it, and let the private sector fund your industrialisation.

## Core mechanics
1. **Military first, economy second** — Open by assessing whether a neighbour will attack; if so, build battalions before any Construction Sector. Congo is safe; Herat needs deterrence vs. Persia; Mascara is actively targeted by France. DECISION RULE: if a great power borders you and dislikes you, your first build order is battalions, not industry. `[00:32]`
2. **Army Power Projection as deterrent** — Hover Prestige to see the Army Power Projection breakdown; raising it makes hostile AI think twice about a Diplomatic Play. DECISION RULE: build *just enough* battalions to discourage a play, not enough to rival the threat — every battalion drives military wages up. `[01:31]`
3. **Always assign Conscripts** — Unassigned conscripts contribute nothing; assigning them adds to Power Projection at zero employment cost until you mobilize. DECISION RULE: every time you check the military tab, click the + on conscripts until they're all assigned. `[03:00]`
4. **Peasant Levies for small nations** — Despite the conventional advice to leave it, Peasant Levies gives -10% military goods cost, +25 Barracks max level, +25 conscription limit, and a 4% conscription rate (vs. 1% on Professional Army). DECISION RULE: stay on Peasant Levies until your economy and arms industry can sustain a >50-battalion army; then switch to Professional Army for the veterancy. `[03:30]`
5. **Mobilization supplements counter morale loss** — Peasant Levies' -10% morale loss is fully offset by any of the three starting supplements (liquor, opium, chocolate, plus tobacco) — each gives +10% morale recovery. DECISION RULE: prioritise liquor, tobacco, opium (½ unit per battalion) over chocolate (1 unit per battalion); enable whichever your Market actually has cheaply. `[10:31]`
6. **Manpower is people, and people are scarce** — Each battalion employs ~1,000 workers who can't staff industry. Small nations cannot afford a large standing army both financially and demographically. DECISION RULE: keep standing army small; rely on conscripts that only consume workers when mobilized. `[07:30]`
7. **Never go into debt as a small nation** — Interest rate scales inversely with Prestige; small nations face ~40% rates that spiral into bankruptcy. Large nations can run healthy debt, you cannot. DECISION RULE: treat zero reserves as a hard floor; if you dip into debt, immediately privatize buildings to climb out. `[14:31]`
8. **Build inputs BEFORE the first Construction Sector** — A Construction Sector consumes 25 fabric + 75 wood per level. Building it before you have cotton plantations and logging camps starts a death spiral. DECISION RULE: build 1 cotton plantation (40 fabric) + 3 logging camps (30 wood each) FIRST, then build your single Construction Sector. `[20:32]`
9. **One Construction Sector is enough at start** — Large nations stack Construction Sectors early; small nations can't sustain the input costs. DECISION RULE: stop at one Construction Sector until private construction or sold buildings give you the reserves to safely add more. `[21:32]`
10. **The Investment Pool bailout** — Once you own at least one Construction Sector, Manor Houses and Financial Districts privately construct buildings funded from the Investment Pool, and you can toggle privatization on any non-government building to receive a lump-sum sale into your reserves. DECISION RULE: when reserves approach zero, sell profitable Urban buildings first (also empowers Industrialists); fall back to rural buildings rather than taking on 40% debt. `[22:31]` `[28:01]`
11. **Build economy in the state with the inputs** — Local prices diverge from the Market price by the Market Access Price Impact (Mappy); under Traditionalism you suffer -15% Mappy, so a state without local supply pays a steep premium. DECISION RULE: site each new building in the same state as its input producers; don't spread industry until Mappy improves. `[24:00]`
12. **Disempower Landowners by selling Urban buildings** — Interest group clout follows wealth. Selling government-built Urban buildings to the private sector hands wealth to Capitalists/Industrialists, breaking the Landowners' grip. DECISION RULE: route privatization toward Urban buildings whenever possible; accept rural sales only as an emergency cash injection. `[28:30]`
13. **Research priority: Stock Exchange or military, then Academia** — Multi-state countries open with Stock Exchange (+10% Mappy). Single-state countries skip it; threatened countries rush military down to General Staff (unlocks General Training, +training rate). Then everyone pivots to Academia for Universities. `[31:01]` `[32:31]` `[35:30]`
14. **Universities multiply Innovation, but cost wages** — Each University level adds +2 Innovation; weeks to research = tech cost ÷ Innovation. They also burn government wages and paper. DECISION RULE: build universities one at a time, confirm your budget absorbs the wages/paper, then add the next. `[35:30]` `[38:00]`
15. **Technology Spread is a free second queue** — You passively research one tech from each tree branch that neighbours already have; you don't choose what spreads. DECISION RULE: don't waste active research on techs already spreading to you when you can check the Technology Spread panel. `[37:02]`
16. **Get off Traditionalism, Serfdom, and Isolationism fast** — Standard advice applies, but Isolationism is especially crippling on a small nation because you can't trade at all. DECISION RULE: prioritise repealing Isolationism above almost any other law change. `[39:01]`

## Game numbers & rules of thumb
- Small-nation interest rates can hit ~40% on debt vs. low rates for great powers; bankruptcy lasts 10 years and scales down over that period. `[15:30]` `[16:31]`
- Peasant Levies: -10% military goods cost, +25 Barracks max level, +25 conscription limit, 4% conscription rate, -10% morale, -25% weekly veterancy gain. `[04:30]` `[05:30]` `[09:32]` `[12:31]`
- Professional Army: +100 Barracks max level, +50 conscription limit, 1% conscription rate. `[07:02]`
- National Guard internal-security law adds +0.5% conscription rate at a bureaucracy cost. `[10:02]`
- Starting Construction Points without a Construction Sector: 10; each Construction Sector adds +2 at wooden buildings PM. `[19:01]` `[19:31]`
- Construction Sector input (wooden buildings PM): 25 fabric + 75 wood per level. `[21:01]`
- Cotton Plantation base output: 40 fabric. Logging Camp base output: 30 wood (more with Sawmills, which needs Steelworking). `[21:32]` `[22:01]`
- A Barracks employs ~1,000 workers per battalion of capacity. `[07:30]`
- Mobilization supplements: liquor / tobacco / opium cost ½ unit per battalion; chocolate costs 1 full unit per battalion; each grants +10% morale recovery. `[11:31]` `[12:02]`
- Each University level adds +2 Innovation; you start with 50 base Innovation; research weeks = tech cost ÷ Innovation. `[36:00]` `[37:02]`
- Traditionalism imposes -15% Mappy and severely throttles Investment Pool contributions. `[23:32]`
- Bankruptcy penalties include -75% offense, -75% defense, -75% prestige, migration attraction loss, Market price impact. `[16:31]`
- A privatized building sale pays a lump sum into reserves (example: a Motor Industry valued at ~£200,000). `[28:01]`
- Irregular Infantry: +10% offense / +10% defense, morale penalty. Line Infantry: +20% offense / +25% defense, needs 1 small arms per battalion. `[32:31]` `[33:00]`

## Common pitfalls
- Building a Construction Sector first as a small nation — the fabric/wood draw on a small Treasury triggers a death spiral before workers are hired.
- Taking debt because "debt is fine in Vic3" — that advice assumes a great-power interest rate, not 40%.
- Stacking Construction Sectors early; you can't feed their inputs and have no Investment Pool yet.
- Switching off Peasant Levies because every guide says Professional Army is better — for a sub-50-battalion army, Peasant Levies is strictly stronger.
- Forgetting to assign Conscripts (free Power Projection just sitting there).
- Building industry in your second state without local inputs and eating the Mappy markup under Traditionalism.
- Refusing to sell agricultural buildings on principle when debt is the alternative — Landowner clout is reversible, a 40% interest spiral is not.
- Spamming Universities for tech catch-up without checking that wages and paper input fit the budget.
- Staying on Isolationism even briefly — the inability to trade is fatal for a small economy.
- Building a standing army big enough to "match" the threat — you'll bankrupt yourself and depopulate your industry; deter, don't match.

## Cheatsheet
1. **Read the threat**: hover Prestige > Army Power Projection. If a great power is hostile, build battalions and assign every Conscript before anything else; otherwise skip to step 2.
2. **Lay economic inputs**: build 1 Cotton Plantation and 3 Logging Camps in your capital state.
3. **Build exactly one Construction Sector** in that same state, then switch the Army Model to Peasant Levies and enable a cheap mobilization supplement (liquor/opium/tobacco).
4. **Research path**: Stock Exchange (multi-state) or military to General Staff (threatened) → then Academia → build Universities one at a time as budget allows.
5. **Keep reserves positive**: as the Investment Pool grows, privatize profitable Urban buildings first (rural only in emergencies) to dump cash into reserves and shift clout to Industrialists.
6. **Repeal Isolationism, Serfdom, and Traditionalism** as early as your Interest Group politics allow; only switch off Peasant Levies once you can financially and demographically sustain >50 battalions.
