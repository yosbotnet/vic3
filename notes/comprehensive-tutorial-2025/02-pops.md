# Pops — Comprehensive Tutorial 2025 (Tarkusarkusar)
**Source:** [The Comprehensive Victoria 3 Tutorial (2025) | Iberian Twilight](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P_PzglCFo9I) by Tarkusarkusar, 2025-12-16 — chapter "2. Pops" [20:48].

## Summary
Pops are the people in your economy; they matter, but less than the economy itself, which is why Tarkus ranks this section second [21:00]. The headline measures of pop success are Standard of Living (SoL) and the loyalist/radical count, both driven mostly by a pop's job [24:00]. A pop's profession determines nearly everything about it — its wage, its Interest Group alignment, and which production methods it can work [25:00] — so you shape your society by building the buildings whose jobs you want [26:31]. Qualifications, literacy, culture/faith, and migration round out the chapter: literacy above ~40% makes qualifications a non-issue [27:30], and migration is gated by cultural communities, markets, and acceptance [30:31].

## Core mechanics
1. **A pop's UI numbers**: population (how many there are), wages, and wage modifiers including "dependent wages" — money earned by the women and children of the family from odd jobs; an abstraction, since in reality only the actual workforce works [21:00]–[22:01].
2. **Slaves — half are workforce**: importing a slave makes the dependents work too (they can't stay home), so you get roughly twice as many workers out of them as out of free pops [22:01]–[22:30].
3. **Slaves don't pay taxes**: the wage a slave would have earned is turned into excess profits for the building's owners instead, and slaves pay no taxes — the big drawback [23:02]–[23:31]. Their SoL is kept survivable only because their owners buy goods for them (that's the small "income" shown, e.g. the "6") [23:02].
4. **Why slavery is bad long-term**: slaves have low SoL, which drags wages down and causes radicalism, and they can't work better jobs — so as production methods advance and demand engineers/high-quality qualifications, slaves become less and less useful [24:00]. Tarkus plans to abolish it [23:31].
5. **SoL is the pop objective**: with respect to pops, success = SoL plus loyalist/radical count (vs GDP for the economy) [24:00]. Pops gain SoL from excess money: at each SoL level they buy goods, then spend remaining money trying to raise SoL; each level consumes more goods [24:31].
6. **Loyalists vs radicals**: when a pop gains a SoL level a fraction become loyalists; when it loses a level a fraction become radicals [24:31]–[25:00].
7. **Two levers for SoL**: (a) make the goods your pops consume cheaper, and (b) make good (better-paying) jobs — Tarkus considers good jobs the more important of the two [24:31]–[25:00].
8. **Jobs determine everything ("communist game")**: a pop's profession determines its wage, its ideology/Interest Group, and which production methods it can work [25:00], [26:00].
9. **IG alignment is from profession**: all pops have a base attraction of 60; profession adds large bonuses — e.g. shopkeeper +150 to Petite Bourgeoisie, engineers +88 to Industrialists (with a small pull to Petite Bourgeoisie too) [25:31]–[26:00].
10. **You engineer society by building**: you control which jobs exist by building the buildings that have them — universities employ academics (empowers intelligentsia), farms make an agrarian society, high-paying jobs for minorities make you multicultural [26:31].
11. **Alignment shifts with society tech**: political alignment changes as you research society techs — e.g. the **Labor Movement** technology makes laborers and machinists start joining Trade Unions; before it they're mostly apolitical or support the Church [27:01].
12. **Qualifications**: generated for pops every week; you get more based on how much literacy your pops have [28:00]–[28:30].
13. **Literacy does three things**: (1) lets pops gain qualifications, (2) raises their minimum required SoL, and (3) decreases their birth rate [28:30].
14. **Why literacy is worth it anyway**: a nation's average literacy determines how quickly it can unlock technology, which outweighs pops wanting more stuff and having fewer kids [28:30].
15. **Cultural communities gate migration**: a pop can only migrate to a location where its culture has a cultural community (except via mass migration); a community means at least one pop of that culture lives there. Communities spawn completely randomly if you have pops of that culture in your market [30:01]–[31:01].
16. **Migration is market-bound**: pops can only migrate among states in the same market (again, except mass migrations) [31:01]–[31:31].
17. **Mass migration**: shown via the mass-migration map mode (green = attractive); pops in low-attraction countries want to move to high-attraction ones. The USA is typically #1 (Canada occasionally close), driven heavily by unused arable land [31:31]–[32:31].
18. **Mass migration needs acceptance**: mass migrations only go to places where the culture would have at least some acceptance [32:31]–[33:00].
19. **Bring in foreign pops the slow way**: with no border controls, conquer states to bring those pops into your market; they may then randomly spawn a cultural community in your states and migrate naturally [33:00].
20. **Obsessions & taboos**: certain cultures/faiths have obsessions (buy more of a good, even at higher prices, to the detriment of other needs) or taboos (rarely buy a good even if cheaper). Religious obsessions/taboos are fixed for the whole game; cultural ones can change [33:31]–[34:01].
21. **Migration decree**: the **Greener Grass Campaign** decree increases immigration attraction; decrees cost authority, which is generated by laws (e.g. monarchy laws) [34:30]–[35:00].

## Numbers & formulas
- Slaves: half of them count as workforce, giving ~twice the workers of free pops [22:01]–[22:30].
- Slave pop income example: shows "6" income — entirely from owners buying goods for them; they otherwise contribute 0 wage/taxes [23:02].
- IG base attraction = 60 for all pops [25:31].
- Profession IG bonuses: shopkeeper +150 to Petite Bourgeoisie [25:31]; engineers +88 to Industrialists [26:00].
- Literacy ~40% threshold: above it you can effectively ignore qualifications entirely; the example country shown has 50% literacy [27:30]–[28:00].
- Only three job tiers exist — laborers, machinists, engineers (capitalists require being rich or an engineer) [27:30].
- Mass migration acceptance bar: you need an acceptance of **30** to receive a culture's mass migration; e.g. a European nation with National Supremacy will never get Chinese mass migrations [33:00].
- Cultural community example: 1,000 Ashkenazi pops = 0.003% of population, but still counts as a community [30:01].
- Belgium example market = Flanders, Wonia, Gallery; pops can only move among these states [31:01]–[31:31].
- Migration-attraction example: Belgium 11.7 (low) vs USA highest; arable land gives unused-arable-land-employment +10, vs Wonia's 0.1 [32:00]–[32:31].

## Common pitfalls
- **Treating slavery as a money meme**: it looks funny/profitable but is bad — slaves pay no taxes, drag wages and SoL down (radicalism), and can't fill advanced jobs as tech progresses [23:31]–[24:00].
- **Overthinking qualifications**: if literacy is above ~40% they're a non-factor; only worry about them in very low-literacy situations like China (Spain/Portugal are lowish but still fine) [27:30]–[29:02].
- **Forgetting market/community gating**: you can't just pull pops from anywhere — they need a cultural community and must be in the same market; conquering or mass migration are the only ways around it [30:31]–[33:00].
- **Expecting to control cultural-community spawns**: they happen randomly and Tarkus admits he doesn't know what influences the odds — in migration-heavy games you just cross your fingers for high-value states [33:00]–[33:31].
- **Ignoring obsessions/taboos when planning trade**: e.g. selling liquor/wine to Muslim-majority pops fails (taboo); Han pops' opium obsession can be exploited (Britain's China opium trade) [33:31]–[34:01].

## Reusable lessons / decision rules
- Optimize pops for **SoL + loyalist/radical count**; optimize the economy for GDP — they are different objectives [24:00].
- Raise SoL with two levers: cheaper consumed goods and better-paying jobs — prioritize **good jobs** [24:31]–[25:00].
- Shape your nation's politics and culture by **building the buildings whose jobs you want** (universities → academics/intelligentsia, farms → agrarian, high-pay jobs → attract minorities) [26:31].
- Don't sweat qualifications when literacy > ~40%; do invest in literacy anyway because average literacy speeds tech, despite higher SoL demands and lower birth rates [28:00]–[28:30].
- Watch society techs (e.g. Labor Movement) that **re-align pops' Interest Groups** over time [27:01].
- For migration: pops are gated by cultural community + same market + acceptance ≥ 30 for mass migration; use conquest (no border controls) and the Greener Grass Campaign decree, then hope for random community spawns [31:01]–[35:00].
- Consider culture/faith **obsessions and taboos** when designing your trade economy; an obsession (e.g. Han opium) can be large enough to run an economy on [33:31]–[34:01].
