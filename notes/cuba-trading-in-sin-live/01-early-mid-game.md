## What happened (early/mid game)
A "trading in sin" Cuba: an economy built on the three Cuban prestige-goods companies the DLC adds — sugar, cigars, and liquor. The opening is deliberately **not** a plantation rush; it spends the first ~40 minutes building the construction/infrastructure backbone (lumber → tools → iron → railways) before the plantation economy is spammed. Migration is pumped with low taxes + the Greener Grass decree + healthcare, drawing French and Mexican mass-migration waves. Cuba takes **Dominion** peacefully via a conscript-bluff, runs cigars + sugar through the Spanish market, then fights an **independence war** (forced by expelling Spanish diplomats), wins it off the back of a Spanish Carlist uprising, hits **Great Power** immediately and bans slavery via the independence journal (unlocking Afro-Caribbean culture). Mid game opens with a **Caribbean Union** power bloc and a string of cheap protectorate wars (New Granada, Venezuela, Bolivia), capped by nationalising all foreign industry and *then* passing Laissez-Faire.

## Decisions by phase

### Phase 1 — The opening: infrastructure before plantations (1836–~1841)
- **Review Cuba's three new prestige companies before unpausing** — sugar (migration attraction + aristocrat IP contribution), liquor, cigars/tobacco. These are what make a plantation-led economy viable now [0:12:31][0:13:01].
- **Build the construction backbone first, not plantations** — 2 construction sectors (one set to agriculture industries, one to resource industries), then lumber camp + iron-tools workshop. "Despite wanting a plantation economy, it's still best to do lumber and tools because we want our construction going." [0:13:30][0:14:31].
- **Queue an iron mine** — tools workshop runs ~11% short without local iron; swap tools to **steel tools** one week before the iron mine finishes to make tools much more profitable [0:21:30][0:29:30].
- **Railways are the acute blocker** — Cuba runs out of infrastructure early; the iron→steel→motors→railway chain must complete before construction sectors can scale. Use the **road maintenance** decree as a stopgap [0:19:01][0:25:31][0:31:30].
- **Decree: Greener Grass Campaigns immediately** (migration attraction) [0:15:00].
- **Research: stock exchange first** ("no-brainer"), then egalitarianism (proportional taxation, universities), then joint-stock for company charters [0:15:00][0:33:30].
- **Request a governor; pick the "influential planter"** (plantation throughput). **Reject "experienced extractor"** despite the strong bonus — its 1% mortality is a hard no, "we don't like losing pops." [0:20:01][0:21:00].
- **Politics:** landowners are strong but happen to be **Reformers** (good). **Do not try to ban slavery yet** — landowner clout is too high to negotiate. Pass **census suffrage** (easy; planters happy). Stay conservative for now [0:15:31][0:32:00].
- **Public healthcare via church-in-government institution** — primarily for migration attraction; level 2 (the +1 migration score) is gated behind Quinine [0:32:30][0:36:31].
- **Diplomacy:** keep Spain friendly to start; declare interests toward **America** (trade) and **England**; **do not join Spain's market** — "Spain doesn't build intelligently, there's no steel in its market," build your own steel chain [0:18:02][0:22:30][0:28:30].
- **Bureaucracy:** queue a bureaucracy building (filing-cabinets PM) when bureaucracy goes negative — "it pays for itself." Don't over-build it before you actually hit the tax-capacity cap (was at full 426) [0:30:00][0:31:30].

### Phase 2 — The sin economy: cigars + sugar (~1841–1849)
- **After railways: spam iron, switch to iron construction, then go heavy on plantations** [0:35:36].
- **Migration waves arrive** — ~71k French in one event, later Mexicans; do nothing to stop them. Cultural communities spread within one market via Greener Grass + ports + RNG; a community must exist in a state before pops migrate there freely [0:38:30][0:40:31].
- **Build tobacco plantations to level 5 in 2+ states → found the cigar company → grant it a tobacco monopoly on investment rights immediately** so foreigners stop buying out tobacco buildings [0:42:00][0:45:30].
- **Use the "exploited rollers" PM** on cigars — more jobs → more migration attraction, worth it even though it needs more labour [0:42:31].
- **Found the sugar prestige company next** for its migration-attraction bonus; grant it a sugar monopoly [0:47:00][0:57:00].
- **~44 trade centres** to export cigars/sugar; stay in the Spanish market for the European migrant flow; plan a **naval interest in North Germany** (Prussia is the biggest cigar importer) once a navy exists [0:58:00][1:06:01].
- **Nationalise the French regional HQ aggressively** (~£450k investments, ~£1M coal mines) to reclaim domestic ownership of your own industry [0:59:32][1:00:02].

### Phase 3 — Dominion without a war (~1849)
- **The conscript bluff:** add conscripts but **never raise them** — this inflates your visible army strength enough that Spain will grant **Dominion** for just an obligation. Dominion = 25% tax vs Colony's 30%, your own diplomacy, and you keep the Spanish market [0:49:01][0:52:00][0:53:02].

### Phase 4 — The independence war (~1850s)
- **Build a navy** (basic + military shipyard) for interest projection and the eventual Haiti landing [0:54:00].
- **Pass Universal Suffrage** — negotiate Church support with political concessions (→ 42% pass), then Proportional Taxation, then remove migration controls (needs the tense intelligentsia — luck-based) [1:02:00][1:08:01][1:19:10].
- **Conquer Haiti under Nationalism** for arable-land relief (Cuba's land is exhausted) plus sugar and dyes [1:14:00][1:21:00].
- **Force the independence war: expel Spanish diplomats** to tank relations so Spain won't grant independence peacefully, then declare with **Puerto Rico** added as a war goal [1:23:02][1:23:30].
- **Win the war:** raise military wages, **drop artillery** (bad early game), **defend Havana before launching the Puerto Rico landing**, then exploit Spain's **Carlist uprising** to land and capitulate them [1:28:00][1:29:00][1:35:02].
- **Independence → immediate Great Power; ban slavery via the independence journal** (unlocks Afro-Caribbean culture); choose **Parliamentary** government [1:38:03][1:38:32].

### Phase 5 — First expansion (post-independence)
- **Form the Caribbean Union power bloc** with the **Cultural Commonwealth** doctrine — lets you grant Afro-Caribbean culture to Romance-speaking subjects for fast incorporation [1:38:32][1:41:31].
- **Cheap protectorate wars:** New Granada, Venezuela, Bolivia — timed around great-power distraction (Austrian/German revolts, Brazil leaving a war first). Capitals on the coast are easy naval-landing wins [1:50:31][2:06:01].
- **Nationalise ALL foreign buildings (~£16M of a £31M credit limit) → THEN pass Laissez-Faire** so foreign owners can't benefit. Order matters; also install a market-liberal agitator in the Petite Bourgeoisie and rig elections for the Liberals first [2:10:02][2:11:00][1:59:32].
- **Stack trade centres in Havana** (higher trade-capacity bonus than Guantánamo) [2:05:00].

## Reusable lessons
- **Infrastructure before plantations.** Even a plantation economy opens with lumber → tools → iron → railways; plantations come after the construction backbone and the infra cap is lifted.
- **Found a prestige company *before* foreigners buy the buildings**, and grant it a monopoly on investment rights the moment it's founded.
- **Dominion is free if you bluff** — conscripts you never raise inflate army strength enough that the overlord grants Dominion for an obligation.
- **Migration is the Cuban engine** — low taxes + Greener Grass + healthcare; let the French/Mexican waves in; communities spread within one market.
- **Conscript early before a war of independence** — the run's biggest self-identified mistake was conscripting too late.
- **Nationalise, then Laissez-Faire** — never the other way, and never switch to Laissez-Faire while still a subject (foreigners buy your industry).

## Pitfalls (narrated)
- Don't build plantations before the construction backbone [0:13:30].
- Don't take the "experienced extractor" governor (1% mortality) [0:21:00].
- Don't join Spain's market (no steel) [0:28:30].
- Don't let foreigners buy your tobacco/sugar before the company exists [0:44:31].
- Don't launch a naval invasion before your capital is defended — Spain landed in Havana while the army was away (reloaded ~2 months) [1:26:30].
- Keep artillery out of the early-game army [1:29:00].
- Don't pass Laissez-Faire before nationalising, or while still a subject [1:20:03][2:10:02].
