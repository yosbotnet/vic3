## Reusable lessons
- **Power Blocks can be formed without vassals.** Sovereign Empire archetype works for a recognized-or-soon-recognized major with surplus influence. Generally: don't assume the prerequisite you remember is still required — re-check the formation tooltip when influence is idle.
- **Defer Power Block activation until you can absorb the -60 relations hit.** Forming early while still grinding recognition tanks the very relations you need.
- **Spend setbacks defensively on critical enactments, not the first stall.** Setbacks are limited; reserve them for late-cycle stalls where failure is near. Track remaining setbacks against expected stall frequency.
- **Foreign-invested companies leak dividends abroad.** Letting a higher-tier GP open a company HQ in your market boosts the building's output but the profits flow to them, not you. Monitor "built by owner" view.
- **Treasury triage hierarchy under interest spiral:** pause construction first, then redirect construction sectors to one PM tier, then trim wages, then raise consumption tax (legitimacy cost), then sell government-owned buildings to private. Avoid more loans.
- **Concede small to keep legitimacy above the enactment floor.** A bad law you can repeal later is cheaper than a stalled reform agenda now.
- **Church/clergy clout is path-dependent.** Religious Schools + mass conversion increases short-term Church power but enables long-term displacement via modernization techs. Plan the sequence, not the snapshot.
- **Concentrate strategic resource mines in the capital when no terrain bonus exists** — local-supply effects compound with the natural building density there.
- **Pre-check law prerequisites for journal-entry completions.** Retire-the-Samurai needs General Training, which needs Warrior Caste repealed first — chain these in the queue, not reactively.
- **Boats need sailors.** Building ships without dedicated Port/sailor-producing buildings leaves a fleet unable to crew. Pair shipyard and port construction.
- **When a foreign GP starts building in your states, that's a signal you've underbuilt the relevant sector.** Either accelerate your own construction or accept the dividend leak.
- **Workaround pattern for character-linked bugs:** merge entities to consolidate state, then split clean — useful when an entity is stuck in an undeletable bad state.
- **Adopt Western diplomatic conventions early if recognition is the bottleneck.** Trading bureaucracy (renewable via tech) for influence/relations/authority is favorable when you're still unrecognized.
