## Reusable lessons
- **You can't protect a subject while you're tied up in another diplomatic play.** If you're already committed elsewhere, expect "failed to protect" auto-independence to wipe fresh conquests. Sequence subject-creation wars so you have free diplomatic bandwidth to defend them during the truce window.
- **Marine + naval-invasion stack is the right tool for small overseas conquests** — instant arrival, no transit risk. Keep marines mobilized and embarked between wars; admirals die often, so plan for re-embarkation.
- **Bureaucracy crises are usually a paper-cost problem, not a building-count problem.** When paper price spikes, every extra GA makes income worse. Trim GAs in over-built states; balance via tax-capacity laws (Appointed Bureaucrats) and by building GAs only where taxation is genuinely bottlenecked.
- **Company picks: optimise for input-goods your economy bleeds on, not for raw output bonuses.** For Japan that means Mitsubishi (paper/iron) and Mitsui (clothing/dyes/silk). Wait for prerequisites rather than burning a slot on a mediocre default.
- **"Support independence" only works if liberty desire is climbing on its own.** Otherwise the play stalls and you've spent influence for nothing. Check the LD trend before committing.
- **You can't reach a country diplomatically without sufficient interest tier in its strategic region.** Build the interest first; expansion plans should include interest-tier prerequisites.
- **Some event options are strictly negative flavour** (e.g. "Brave Engineer" losing infrastructure). Read them, don't auto-click.
- **Enactment stall rates feel non-intuitive** — high-success enacts often stall, low-success ones pass. Don't gate strategy on a single enactment attempt.
- **Mass unemployment with no employable buildings is a danger sign** — possibly a bug after patches, but more generally: building construction must keep pace with pop growth, or radicals spiral past any law/edict you can throw at them. Once a pop class is mass-radicalised it self-sustains regardless of standard-of-living interventions.
- **Player's own retrospective in this episode:** suspects post-patch save state is broken (game "broke down because of updates and patches" `[32:00]`). Worth checking if a fresh save reproduces before blaming strategy. Take the screenshot, investigate via console — don't fight a phantom economic crisis with in-game tools that can't address it.
