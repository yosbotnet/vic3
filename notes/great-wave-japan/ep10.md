## Reusable lessons
- **Restoration / unique national journals have implicit law prerequisites that are not obvious from the journal text.** Check law-change requirements early; the Warrior Caste swap requires Lex Edo Status, which itself requires fighting the dominant landed IG.
- **Trigger major reform journals only when the country can actually pass the prerequisite laws.** Doing it "too early" (before the dominant IGs have shifted) makes the journal effectively uncompletable in its time window.
- **Bolster a movement rather than give an IG clout/concessions** when you want enactment speed but not lasting political debt — bolster is reversible, clout grants are not.
- **Use law concessions strategically when the concession self-destructs.** Granting "political concessions to Lex Edo Status" is free once Lex Edo Status passes; pick concessions whose value collapses with the law you are enacting.
- **Cancel treaties that bind an opposing IG into an angry interest league.** Removing the treaty removes the league, removes the radicals. Cheaper than fighting the IG directly.
- **Enact an intermediate law (Landed Voting) to reshuffle clout before attempting a harder law.** Constitutional monarchy + favorable election + cabinet packing can shift the math on a law that was otherwise unpassable.
- **Cabinet composition is a lever, not a default.** Deliberately exclude IGs that would radicalize, include all IGs that support the law — even if it leaves the cabinet politically awkward elsewhere.
- **Keep individually-useful characters even if their IG is hostile.** A pro-law leader inside an anti-law IG is more valuable than ideological purity.
- **Temporary tax cuts during enactment exploit the law-speed modifier cheaply.** Restore after the law passes.
- **Prioritize Gentry-pool dividend tech to offload construction to private investment.** Frees state construction for journal/critical-path buildings.
- **Tooling and steel bottlenecks gate everything urban.** Queue steel mills and tooling workshops before mass-queuing universities or upgraded buildings, and use queue reordering to push them to the front.
- **Universities cost 400 construction and only 20 urbanization** — a cheap qualification booster compared to most urban upgrades.
- **Skip nice-to-have buildings (e.g., naval administrations) during a tight journal timer.** Opportunity cost of construction is the entire restoration window.
- **Literacy loss from annexed territory is acceptable** — it does not directly slow research, only tech spread; the new pops are still net positive.
- **Suppressed coups still drain political support; expect them when reforming against an entrenched IG.** Plan enactment around the radical/coup risk window.
