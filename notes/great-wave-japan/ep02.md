# Episode 2 — Resolving the Tempo Crisis: wood, tools, and police

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=53Y3n43jiiE)

## What happened
Working under the Tempo Crisis timer with Japan isolated from the world market, the player focused on cheapening construction (wood + tools), nibbling away at the Daimyo's law-enactment penalties, and pushing through Dedicated Police Force. A National Guard enactment was started to buff the Chonin, the army was trimmed to free upkeep, and rice/grain expansion began in Kanto and Osaka to hit urbanization and food agendas.

## Decisions made
- **Build construction sectors in resource-rich states (Kyushu, Shikoku)** rather than Kanto [00:30, 29:30]. Reasoning: Kyushu has iron+gold for cheap steel later; Shikoku has 16 iron mines which will matter when production methods upgrade. Skipped Kanto-only build because iron supply is the bottleneck.
- **Spam logging camps first, then a tooling workshop, then iron mine, then sawmill PM, then iron-tools PM** [02:00, 05:35, 07:30, 10:30, 14:31]. Reasoning: construction goods are wood-dominant; cheaper wood directly drops build cost. Tools double logging output but require iron, so the order is forced. Result: construction cost dropped from ~32k to ~22k [27:31].
- **Co-locate tooling workshop with iron mine in the same state (Kanto)** [08:31]. Reasoning: avoid market-price penalty since isolated Japan has weak goods-price spread (only 65% market access tech).
- **Disable fish-fertilizer PM on rice farms; keep simple farming** [07:00]. Reasoning: 10 fish in for 10 grain out is a bad trade when fig orchards already supply sugar for grocery chains.
- **Fire 7 battalions down to 51** [09:00]. Reasoning: Tempo Crisis requires 500 Power Projection = 50 battalions at 10/each; everything above is wasted upkeep while Japan is locked in and can't attack.
- **Subsidize ports, iron mine, tooling workshop temporarily** [03:30, 28:31]. Reasoning: ports produce unused merchant marine but are needed for supply ships; iron/tools need to staff up urgently and shopkeepers refused current wages.
- **Use a setback on Dedicated Police Force enactment for +10% pass chance** [05:02]. Reasoning: trades a popularity hit on a non-critical character for materially better odds on a load-bearing law. Succeeded [19:32].
- **Reject swapping cabinet to put Chonin in once they hit Influential** [06:30]. Reasoning: it would have pissed off the Daimyo and doubled stall chance from 25% to 50%. Kept Daimyo neutral; postponed Chonin promotion until they could be added alongside.
- **Enact National Guard next** [20:30, 22:32]. Reasoning: gives Chonin political strength bonus (path to 15–20% IG buff at higher levels), and only the Sabaku peasant movement opposes — and they sit below the 25% activism threshold for stall.
- **Accept Chonin petition for Capital Fleets** [25:00]. Reasoning: refusing costs 5 legitimacy + 2 authority and risks losing treasury bonds; the 5-year deadline is plausible.
- **Accept Peasant Movement petition (lower enact time, but boost peasant attraction)** [26:00]. Reasoning: deliberately feed peasant movement to later use it as the vehicle for abolishing Serfdom.
- **Privatize everything; let investment pool buy buildings** [24:00]. Reasoning: stated long-term plan — go full privatization while keeping the Emperor; treat Japan as an industrialized empire run by capital.
- **Build paper mill before universities** [33:31]. Reasoning: paper price needs to come down first or universities are too expensive to run at scale.
- **Build at least one rice farm per state for local grain** [35:00]. Reasoning: grain is traded locally, not pooled; per-state shortages exist even with national surplus.

## Outcomes
- Dedicated Police Force passed [19:32], removing 5% of the law-enactment penalty stack — directly enabled by the setback gamble and by not antagonizing Daimyo.
- Construction cost fell ~30% (32k → 22k) [27:31] within the episode once wood + tools chain was online.
- Tools shortage briefly recurred [27:02] because workshops failed to hire at base wages — fixed by subsidies.
- Cabinet juggling kept Daimyo neutral (0.5x stall) instead of hostile (1.0x stall), preserving the 25% stall chance on subsequent laws.
- National Guard enactment running with no stall chance because Sabaku activism stayed under 25% [23:02].
- Cotton Gin researched first [17:30]; literacy 77% (rank 1 globally) noted but underused since only 50 of 165 possible innovation captured — no universities yet.

## Reusable lessons
- **Sequence construction-cheapening before anything ambitious**: logging camps → tooling workshop → iron mine → enable sawmill + iron-tools PMs. The build-cost drop compounds across every later project.
- **Co-locate input chains in one state** when market access tech is weak (early game, isolated nations, low infrastructure). The state-vs-market price gap can wipe out the apparent profit.
- **Trim the army to the exact Power Projection threshold** during forced-peace phases. Battalions above the requirement are pure upkeep drain.
- **Setbacks are worth spending on load-bearing laws.** A +10% pass chance on a law that unlocks further laws is a better trade than preserving a single character's popularity.
- **Don't promote a newly-Influential IG into cabinet if it costs you a neutral powerful IG.** Stall chance is multiplicative; doubling it via an angry IG often outweighs the IG-strength gain.
- **Pick the next law by who opposes it, not by what it does.** Look for laws where opposition movements are below the 25% activism threshold — those enact with no stall penalty.
- **Feed petitions you'd accept anyway**: accepting an IG's petition is cheap legitimacy/relations; the cost of refusal (authority, legitimacy, treasury bonds) usually exceeds the marginal cost of compliance.
- **Cultivate the movement you'll later need.** Boosting Peasant Movement attraction now sets up Serfdom abolition later — political movements are tools, not nuisances.
- **Subsidize buildings that won't hire at base wages** during the bootstrapping phase. Losing money briefly is fine if it gets a critical input flowing; revoke subsidies once the wider economy catches up.
- **Build prerequisite-good industries before the consumers.** Paper before universities; tools before everything; wood before construction sectors. Cheap inputs make the downstream affordable.
- **Per-state goods (grain, services) need per-state production.** Don't rely on the national balance — check local prices.
- **Disable PMs that trade scarce inputs for marginal outputs** (e.g., fish-fertilizer rice). Re-evaluate every PM when inputs are constrained.
