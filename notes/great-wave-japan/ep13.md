# Episode 13 — Food crisis, frigates for transport, Prussian defensive pact

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep13](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T4WXyfWsanc)

## What happened
Episode is dominated by cost pressure: groceries are eating the lowest strata's budget, paper and iron are the government's two biggest expenses, and a disease outbreak then food-price spike pushes the empire into ~-21k weekly with 10-11k of that being interest on debt. The player builds out fertilizer + groceries to stabilise food, declines a flavour decision that would have raised paper demand, and pivots naval construction from ships-of-the-line to frigates/cruisers to grow naval transport capacity ahead of a planned Korea invasion. Diplomacy: secures a defensive pact with Prussia after maxing relations. Multiple failed enact attempts on Per Capita Taxation (4 setbacks in a row), but the law's threshold condition is finally crossed late episode.

## Decisions made
- **Reject "Japanese become obsessed with paper" event option [01:32]** — alternatives were "make monks better (more bureaucracy)" vs. the obsession modifier. Chose the bureaucracy boost. Reasoning: paper is a *government good*; raising pop demand raises the price the state itself pays. Already spending 38k on paper, second-highest line item after iron (~46k).
- **Build out paper supply to push price to "normal" [03:00]** — identified iron and paper as the two largest state expenditures via the government goods view. Rule applied: for goods the government consumes heavily, keeping market price at-or-below normal is worth more than the equivalent profit-seeking build.
- **Switch naval construction from ship-of-the-line to frigates, then cruisers [07:31, 17:00]** — alternatives: keep building SoL (0.55 carrying capacity each), wait for the locked troop-ship tech, or build frigates (0.40 carrying, much cheaper) / cruisers (cheaper, faster build, higher carrying-per-time). Reasoning: only 13 troop transport capacity currently, and Korea invasion requires sealift. Frigates/cruisers reach capacity faster per construction-week than SoL.
- **Frigate template — drop speed, max other stats [22:02]** — set speed to minimum because existing fleet caps at 9.5 and new ships will be bottlenecked to fleet speed anyway. Spent the points on screening / hull / carrying. General rule: ship-design points spent on stats above the fleet bottleneck are wasted.
- **Accept Promote Social Mobility decree [05:02]** — literacy is decaying because the 1.13.1 patch made literacy decay toward education access. Decree increases literacy access to counter the new decay floor.
- **Stay the course on Per Capita Taxation despite 4 enactment setbacks [16:31, 31:00]** — alternatives: swap to easier law. Reasoning: roughly at the 70% peasant threshold where per-capita starts to outperform land-based, because employed-pop tax base is now large enough. Land-based halves peasant tax and adds tax on all non-peasants; the swing point is when non-peasant income share exceeds the lost peasant tax.
- **Refuse Dutch defensive pact (with food-tribute clause) [25:33]** — wouldn't pay the price in food given the ongoing food crisis.
- **Take Prussian defensive pact + trade privileges + obligation [33:03]** — paid an obligation to lock it in for max duration. Reasoning: only diplomatic lever available to grow Overlapping Interests (currently can't reach level 3 in any of Prussia's areas). Trade privileges chosen because they make Prussian-exported goods cheaper to import.
- **Continue military research during the "research penalty" window, banking for the bonus [19:33]** — there's a -% modifier active for ~3 more years followed by a + bonus window. Sequencing research to put the more important techs inside the bonus window.
- **Skip Health System tech branch in favour of more military tech [18:30]** — explicit choice; war phase approaching, food crisis already self-correcting via fertilizer.
- **Privatize fertilizer plants on completion [08:31]** — standard play: state builds, then hands to capitalists once profitable to free construction capacity / get dividend income to the state via owned shares.
- **Lower relations with Korea [06:32]** — preparing CB / war goal pathway for the planned invasion.
- **Halt new construction queue while bleeding cash [25:00, 28:01]** — even with construction paused the deficit is severe (-21k) because private construction sector still consumes goods that the state effectively subsidises via market prices; pausing state queue alone doesn't fix it.

## Reusable lessons
- **Audit government goods, not just market profitability.** Sort/inspect the state's own purchase line items; the top 1-2 goods (often paper, iron, small arms, services) deserve dedicated state-built supply specifically to crush their *price*, not to make profit. The saving on government spend dwarfs the building's own balance sheet.
- **Decline events that raise pop demand for a good the state is a net buyer of.** Pop "obsession" / cultural-demand modifiers are a tax on the treasury whenever the good in question is a government good you're already short on.
- **Match naval construction to the bottleneck you actually have.** If the limiting factor is transport capacity for an upcoming invasion, optimise carrying-capacity-per-construction-week, not per-hull. Cheaper frigates/cruisers usually win until troop-ship tech unlocks.
- **Ship designer: don't pay for speed above the fleet's slowest hull.** New ships inherit the fleet's speed cap, so points above that cap are wasted. Reinvest into stats that compose (screening, hull, carrying).
- **Per Capita Taxation has a peasant-share crossover point (~70%).** Below it, land tax + consumption is fine. Above it (i.e. once enough pops are employed in non-peasant buildings), per-capita yields more *and* relieves the peasant SoL during war. Persisting through enactment failures is correct once the crossover is reached.
- **Pausing state construction does not fix a deficit caused by private construction.** Private construction sector consumes goods at market price; if those goods are expensive due to state imports, the deficit persists even with an empty state queue. Real fix is supply expansion or letting prices settle.
- **Time research toward modifier windows.** When facing a temporary research malus followed by a bonus, queue high-value techs to land inside the bonus window; spend the malus window on cheaper prerequisites.
- **Patch 1.13.1 literacy decay:** literacy now decays toward education access. If literacy falls without an obvious cause, raise education access (Promote Social Mobility, schooling laws, religious-academia institutions) — the previous "literacy is sticky" intuition is obsolete.
- **Defensive pacts add only modest overlapping-interest score (~400).** They aren't a shortcut to alliance-grade involvement scores (~1000+); plan additional levers (subject relationships, shared wars, trade privileges) if you need a power-bloc partner far from your home region.
- **Distance to Europe shrinks trade-route value.** Trade route profitability scales inversely with distance to the world market hub; Japan's exports to European GPs are marginal even when the goods are competitive.
- **Watch interest as a share of expenses.** Once debt interest exceeds ~10% of weekly expenses, new construction is self-defeating — prioritise paying down or raising taxes before resuming the build queue.
