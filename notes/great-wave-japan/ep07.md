**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep7](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pi4LasUVna0)

## What happened

The Tokugawa shogunate was abolished, Japan became the Empire of Japan under an oligarchy, and the Bakumatsu modifier started. Side work: paper-mill build-out to lower paper price, shipyard/artillery rebalancing, annexation of Ezoki vassal, and a clothing build-up to soften an incoming radicalization wave.

## Reusable lessons
- **Unrecognized status = no debt tolerance.** 20–26% interest makes any borrowing destroy your budget. Cut treaty upkeep and port subsidies before cutting construction.
- **Lower local prices, don't import.** For institution inputs (paper) and standard-of-living goods (clothing), distributing production across states beats relying on the world market — local production modifiers compound and avoid trade-route costs.
- **Force production via a demand floor.** When auto-AI won't build a good because demand is fractional (artillery, specialized military goods), manually order a tiny amount to push demand above the threshold.
- **Movement activism is a tool, not a side effect.** You can deliberately enact laws a movement opposes to spike its activism toward the threshold needed for a journal-entry click. Trade Privileges treaties are a reliable xenophobe-activism lever.
- **Read the legitimacy gate before clicking irreversible buttons.** Same button, different shogun legitimacy → different branch outcomes. Save before, test the branch, reload if wrong. The "unforeseen consequences" warning on low legitimacy is the *desired* path for the restoration arc.
- **Use Setbacks deliberately.** Movement-driven law changes that are about to fail can be set back to increase next-attempt success chance rather than letting them fail outright.
- **Stage SoL buffers before known radicalization events.** When a 5-month timer warns of mass radicalization, build the goods that dominate the affected pops' consumption basket — small price drops translate directly into fewer radicals.
- **Annex/incorporate cheap vassals for journal triggers.** Subject states often gate journal entries that need "own all of X" or "urban center in X"; incorporation is usually a free GDP/admin upgrade.
- **Don't subsidize losers by default.** Port/building subsidies are easy to forget; treat them as discretionary spend and cut first under fiscal pressure.
- **Naval power projection scales by tonnage tiers (e.g. 2500), not linearly.** Build to the next breakpoint, not incrementally, when chasing a projection level.
