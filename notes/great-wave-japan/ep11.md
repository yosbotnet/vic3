## Reusable lessons
- **Stalled enactment outcomes are pre-determined per tick.** Reloading and re-clicking on the same in-game day yields the same roll. To change the outcome you must change the stall-chance modifier *before* the next monthly tick (negotiate with an IG leader, sway, or accept costly demands), then advance time.
- **Composing a cabinet around the next law, not the last law, is usually correct.** When the blocker is a single IG (e.g. Kazoku opposing Professional Army), reshape the government to exclude only that IG; the same composition often unblocks a chain of related reforms (Professional Army then End Edo System here).
- **Pick unit upgrades by input chain, not paper stats.** Going one tier up the cheap-input branch (Line Infantry on Small Arms) is faster to deploy than skipping to a higher tier that needs goods you don't yet produce at scale (Skirmish needing Ammunition).
- **Government Administrations are the urbanization speedrun building.** Cost ~250, build very fast, +20 urbanization per level, downgradable. When a journal requires urbanization tier 5 in a tight window, spam GAs across qualifying states and cancel the surplus after the threshold pings.
- **Compare urbanization-per-construction-point, not per-level.** Art Academy and University give +20 urbanization at 400 construction; GA gives the same +20 at 250. The cheapest building per urbanization point is the right one for journal deadlines.
- **State Religion is a reversible tool, not a permanent identity.** Adopting it to satisfy a journal and start a conversion threshold is fine — plan the unwind law (Total Separation / State Atheism) for after the threshold is met, so the bonus persists without the ongoing penalty.
- **Sovereign Empire / Vassalization power blocks pair with the Great Power transition.** Forming the block right after recognition compounds influence and leverage benefits and gives a credible expansion tool before formal conquest tech.
- **Coup commander retirement can backfire.** Retiring the named commander has a chance to immediately trigger the coup it was meant to defuse — weigh against alternatives (assign them to a distant theatre, accept the agitator).
- **Great Power status drops loan interest noticeably** (here to ~7.5%), so any planned spending spike (construction wave, military buildout) is cheapest right after recognition.
- **Island starts are transport-capped.** Even after becoming a Great Power, overseas subjugation requires a fleet that can move 10+ units; plan Convoy/transport build well before declaring interest in distant markets.
