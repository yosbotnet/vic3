# Episode 8 — Komei Restoration and the Mission to the West

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep8](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eiMUXGkmWDo)

## What happened
The Imperial faction wins the political debate just before a Sonno Joi uprising fires, completing the Komei Restoration: Bakufu falls, capital relocates to Kanto, daimyo convert into the Kazoku (losing Bakuhan, gaining Hierarchic). Three Restoration journal entries open with a 12-year clock: Dispatch a Mission to the West, Industrialize Japan, and Retire the Samurai. The Maeda mission is sent abroad and tours Mexico, Britain, France, Netherlands, Prussia, Moldavia/Balkans, Ottomans, Greece, and Ceylon, returning with research bonuses and Public Schools. In parallel the player begins a railway buildout, foreign investment treaty with Austria, Frontier Colonization, and Shinbutsu Bunri, while surviving a second coup attempt by unassigning the general.

## Reusable lessons
- **Save before a likely uprising tick.** Picking the wrong side of a civil war is effectively unrecoverable; a manual save is the cheapest insurance.
- **On debate / journal-entry ticks, always click the highest-success option** — they're independent rolls and small EV gains compound over many months.
- **Restoration-style ally choices: optimise for law preferences, not the +50% IG strength.** A 50% boost to a marginal IG matters less than aligning their preferred laws with your endgame law path.
- **Pick ambassadors / envoys by trait synergy with the mechanic's reward**, not by current prominence. Sick + low-prominence is fine if the event grants traits that turn them into a modernizer/diplomat.
- **Mission-to-West-style country tours: short stops in low-template nations, long stops in template nations.** Stay long where the laws/institutions match your goal state.
- **Prefer institution unlocks (e.g. Public Schools) over flat research-speed bonuses** when both are offered — institutions compound, modifiers don't.
- **Foreign investment rights are a cheap diplomatic currency** when you have no alliance access; you can nationalise the buildings later. Accept the IG approval cost as the price of geopolitical insurance.
- **Sequence reforms by radicalisation cost, not by importance.** Push non-radicalising laws (e.g. Frontier Colonization) ahead of contentious ones so the radical budget is preserved for the laws that actually anger IGs.
- **Coup mitigation without combat: unassign the rebelling general.** A general with no command produces no coup progress. Keep field armies split so no single commander has decisive force.
- **Run the journal-entry clock and the law-change clock in parallel from day one.** A 12-year restoration timer assumes you're already enacting, building, and researching simultaneously — sequential play will miss the window.
- **Audit core states for missing baseline buildings (tooling workshops, engine plants) when the economy stalls** — silent shortages cap construction throughput more than visible deficits do.
