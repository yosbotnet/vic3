# Episode 4 — Tempo Crisis reform path and Edo-Osaka confiscation

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s2zLZ5dM0CY)

## What happened
Still in the Honorable Restoration period, the player works the Tempo Crisis journal entry along the reformer agenda to push Chonin (petite bourgeoisie) clout above 10 and unlock Imperial Restoration. Capital-Fleets law is enacted (with stalls and a costly Church concession), the Edo-Osaka Confiscation Order is fired off the back of new naval fortifications in Kanto and Osaka, and farming/tooling/iron infrastructure is queued in preparation for the atmospheric engine and Iron-Frame Buildings PM swap. The shogun heir becomes an adult moderate, opening a future path to petition for Imperial Restoration without forcing the Sonno Joi movement above 50%.

Reasoning: petite bourgeoisie are petitioning for it (free attraction), capital ships will be needed once atmospheric engine arrives in ~3 years, and the rural folk penalty is acceptable when the player doesn't depend on them.

## Reusable lessons
- **Pick the Tempo-Crisis-equivalent side that buffs the interest group you actually want to empower.** Reformer vs hardliner choices are about which IG gets the attraction bonus, not flavour.
- **Time interest-group law petitions to enact "free" laws.** When an IG petitions for a law you were going to pass anyway, enact it — you get the attraction with no opportunity cost. Don't pay an unrelated concession for what the situation is offering.
- **Don't pay Tense interest groups to enter government for an enactment boost unless the law is failing.** Their concession demands scale with their mood; the boost is rarely worth the multi-year debuff. Try without first; reserve the bribe as a save move.
- **Patronizing pops with Authority is bad value at low prominence.** ~0.1-0.2% per 100 Authority. Use Authority for decrees, social mobility, and bolstering movements instead.
- **Never commit to a Standard-of-Living target you can't hit within the modifier's lifetime.** A failed/partial SoL commitment leaves the interest group huge attraction for years and feeds opposition agitators. Take the cheaper concession even if the % boost looks smaller.
- **Stack decree-cost reducers (Imperious ruler, Consolidation Act, etc.) before spamming decrees.** A state at 40-cost decrees absorbs many more buffs per Authority point than a state at 100+.
- **Build prerequisite infrastructure (Naval Fortifications, etc.) on speculative timing for journal/decision triggers.** The unlock often has a payback period far shorter than the build time, especially in high-value states.
- **On centralization/landholder-resistance events, optimize against the IG you're trying to weaken, not for the cheapest gold cost.** Picking the option that buffs the daimyo/landowners to save money undoes the whole point of the centralization play.
- **Switch construction PMs only after stockpiling new inputs.** Iron-Frame Buildings need iron and tools; queue the mines/workshops before the swap so construction throughput doesn't collapse.
- **Movement activism must be above 25% for its support % to count as the law's success chance.** Bolster the one movement that crossed the threshold rather than spreading Authority across several stuck below it.
- **Track multiple unlock paths for the same goal.** Imperial Restoration here unlocks via either Sonno Joi >50% activism OR a non-Traditionalist shogun — an adult moderate heir is a second clock running in parallel and changes which laws are worth picking fights over.
- **Tiered diplomatic interest from "Improve Relations" is a cheap way to project into a region** without paying for a permanent strategic interest — useful for opportunistic plays.
- **When the navy is dedicated to supply ships, accept that overseas plays are off the table** and don't start them. Plan the law/PM order so the transport fleet exists before you need to use it.
