# Episode 5 — Racing the Honorable Restoration timer

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep5](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QDwEtDNWTnU)

## What happened

The bottleneck is now Petite Bourgeoisie political strength (target ~10%, currently ~9%) and getting rid of Hereditary Bureaucrats before the Daimyo's mood window closes. The Shogun is increasingly worried the run will fail the agenda inside the remaining ~2 years 10 months.

## Reusable lessons
- **Read modifier durations before acting on law changes.** The Modifiers tab shows exact remaining months — use it to time enactments so the IG mood window covers the stall period.
- **Naval fortifications scale by count, not coverage.** Stack them in your biggest/most productive states to also get bombardment/blockade/invasion-defense where it matters.
- **Shipyards have no input shortage penalty.** You can mass-produce hulls before Artillery/Engines supply chains are ready — only the price of subsidizing the yard matters, not throughput.
- **Match enemy armor tier on capital ships.** If both sides have equal armor, neither penetrates — a cheap defensive posture against a stronger naval rival.
- **Promoting Petite Bourgeoisie clout is a pop-and-cabinet problem, not a click problem.** Once magnates max prominence (100) and own all available holdings, the only levers left are: (a) Peerage/Patronize to move PB characters into noble-eligible cabinet slots, and (b) build Furniture/Textile/Paper to grow Shopkeeper pops. Compare buildings by employees-per-level of the target profession before committing construction.
- **Build Coal Mines the moment Atmospheric Engine is on the horizon.** Mine PMs and ship engines both demand coal; forgetting blocks the whole industrial chain.
- **Subsidize, don't cancel, a shortage-bound input mine while upgrading it.** Keeps employment + lets the tier-up resolve the shortage organically.
- **Always keep a backup agenda path.** If the primary clout/law target is slipping, scout an alternative trigger (here: rigid status order via deliberately provoking an IG over 27.5%) so the run isn't binary.
- **Decline foreign "build in your country" requests by default in early-mid game.** Domestic ownership keeps dividends in the investment pool you control.
- **Eat small -1 IG approval modifiers from refusing ultimatums** rather than locking yourself into permanent factional submission.
- **Don't skip Innovation upgrades during research unlocks** (Dialectics → Universities) — passive science output compounds and is the cheapest source of long-run tech tempo.
- **Queue the next hull immediately when a shipyard finishes a ship.** Empty shipyards waste the timer window and the input chain you just stood up.
