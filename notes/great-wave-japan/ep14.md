## Decisions made
- **Concede to revolution with Census Suffrage [01:34–02:01]**: Radicals' revolution had progressed too far to stall. Both active movements (Liberal + Radical) listed census suffrage as a demand → single law change defuses two movements. Alternative (wealth voting) only satisfied one. Rule: when conceding, pick the law that satisfies the maximum number of active movements.
- **Save-scum and abort Taiwan war [04:02–15:39]**: Initial reasoning was "Qing has no big navy → Taiwan undefended." Discovered mid-war: the target state was tagged South China with 270 garrisoned troops, plus own navy could not maintain organisation. Reloaded rather than press a doomed invasion. Lesson: verify state-region ownership and garrison size before declaring; "small navy" does not imply "no land troops in coastal state."
- **Build Landing Marines for invasions [03:05–03:31]**: Same combat strength as skirmishers but no naval-invasion penalty. Cost: ammunition + small arms (requires ammunition factories online). Generalisation: once ammunition is producible, swap skirmishers for landing marines in any army intended for amphibious work.
- **Pre-build ammunition before army upgrades [33:30–34:30]**: Sequenced explosives → ammunition factories before swapping unit types so demand can be met domestically. 1 ammunition per battalion; 2 factories (~100 units) gave wide margin.
- **One grocery industry per state [00:32–01:01]**: Local supplies are state-local, so distributing groceries reduces local price spikes faster than concentrating one mega-industry. Same logic applies to any consumer good driving pop SoL shortfalls.
- **Hokkaido circuit pick: Hokkaido / North Sea [19:33–21:31]**: Chose assimilation + agricultural bonus over the Hitakami-do option (loyalty + romanticism IG boost). Reasoning: assimilation of Ainu is a permanent demographic gain; loyalty modifiers expire and romanticism wasn't desired. Generalisation: prefer permanent/structural rewards (assimilation, building slots, arable land) over modifier-only rewards from journal-entry forks.
- **Settle Hokkaido via armed-forces colonists [22:36–23:30]**: Chose armed-forces settler option over peasant assimilation route — military IG was needier and the migration % was comparable. Decision rule: tie journal-entry IG rewards to whichever IG currently most needs loyalty.
- **Decline election meddling [27:01]**: Already meddled previously; stacking meddles triggers escalating penalties. Skipped this cycle to bank goodwill.
- **Cancel laws + accept 60% stall cabinet [33:02–33:30]**: With Intelligentsia excluded from cabinet, only governments with 60% stall chance were available. Took the gamble rather than wait another full election cycle — law-change throughput is the binding constraint on the run.
- **Delay foreign-obligation commitment [26:12–26:32]**: Announce-delay used rather than fail outright; preserves diplomatic standing while law changes are stuck.
- **Found Sapporo Agricultural College via university build [23:30–24:32]**: Required prerequisite. New-crops action then unlocks rice (best food good), indigo, opium, vineyards on Hokkaido — converts the wheat-only frontier into a multi-crop agri base.

## Reusable lessons
- **Stack movement demands on one law change**: when forced to concede, identify the law that overlaps the most active movements before picking.
- **Pre-war checklist**: state-region ownership (the state your war goal sits in may be a different tag's territory), garrison count, naval supply throughput simulation, strait adjacency for enemy land-army travel. "Weak navy" ≠ "weak coastal defence."
- **Save before declaring wars on novel targets** so unexpected garrisons or mechanics can be reverted without diplomatic cost.
- **Sequence consumable-dependent unit unlocks**: build the input factory (ammunition, small arms, explosives) before retraining troops, or organisation/combat will collapse on first engagement.
- **Local-supply goods scale by spreading thin**: one factory per state for consumer goods (groceries, furniture, clothing) beats one concentrated mega-factory for SoL relief.
- **Journal-entry forks**: prefer permanent rewards (assimilation, building slots, arable land, new crops) over time-limited modifiers (loyalty, IG attraction).
- **Election strategy**: meddle sparingly; consecutive meddles compound penalties. Accept worse cabinets if law-change throughput is the bottleneck — waiting an election cycle is often more expensive than a 60% stall chance.
- **Use "announce delay" on diplomatic commitments** rather than failing them when domestic politics are jammed.
- **Naval supply diagnostics**: assigned ≠ delivering. Even with surplus shipyards and a single consumer fleet, throughput can stall for reasons not surfaced in tooltips. Worth verifying via small test fleets before committing to overseas invasions.
- **Taming the North template**: applies to any frontier-development journal entry — build urban centre to trigger, then university → agricultural college → crop/reclamation action loops; check pop and GDP gates early.
