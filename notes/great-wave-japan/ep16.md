## Reusable lessons
- **Appointed Bureaucrats unlocks taxation capacity** — if you're capping taxes well below need, this is the law to chase; success chance is fragile against election-driven government reshuffles, so endorse the backing IG before voting and avoid taking party sides that fracture the supporting coalition.
- **Elections can soft-reset law progress** by changing the legitimate government mid-vote. Time critical law pushes between elections, or accept that you'll cancel and restart.
- **Protectorate-first expansion against weak neighbours** gives income, a power-block member, and a path to later annexation at lower infamy than direct conquest. Threats often back down with zero fighting.
- **Marines bypass invasion penalty timer** but are capacity-limited; build enough transports + supply ships before committing, and split the fleet so marines embark without dragging the whole navy off station.
- **Co-locate military unit production with their input-building states** to keep supply costs sane.
- **For journal-entry rewards, prefer permanent (research/tech) over temporary buffs.**
- **Defusing a movement = giving them the direction they want, not the maximal tier.** Cheap law changes can dissolve revolution risk even if the new law is mediocre on its merits.
- **Granting foreign investment rights inside your country leaks dividends abroad** — vet which country and which buildings before approving construction rights.
- **National-value conversions are irreversible** — confirm path before spending authority.
- **Power-block income + protectorates is a viable economic outlet when domestic construction is starving the budget**, since it doesn't require new construction spend.
- **Supply-ship scarcity is a hard ceiling on naval ops**; assign supply priority to the offensive force, not the patrol fleet, when scarce.
