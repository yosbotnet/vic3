# Episode 15 — Skirmish infantry, landing marines, per capita taxation, Meiji succession

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep15](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2QnyrQyPlso)

## What happened

Patch 1.13.3. Fleet sent home to recover supplies lost when the admiral died. Upgraded land army from line infantry to skirmish infantry once first ammunition production came online. Built a dedicated 20-unit landing-marine invasion force to avoid the naval-invasion-efficiency penalty other unit types carry. Per Capita Taxation enacted (fulfilling a Goshu promise), Law Enforcement reached level 3, Nitroglycerin unlocked and rolled out to mines/explosives. The Emperor in Parliament journal entry resolved into Responsible Government. Heir came of age as a Goshu modernizer. Mid-episode the Emperor died at 37; the new Meiji emperor reset interest-group dynamics and pushed the player back into illegitimate-government territory while attempting Appointed Bureaucrats.

## Decisions made

- **Send fleet to port to recover supplies after admiral death** `[00:30]` — confirmed by player as the fix; cost: temporary loss of power-projection interest income. Generalisation: an admiral's death wipes fleet supply; dock to recover rather than expecting it to refill at sea.
- **Subsidize new ammunition + explosives factories briefly to trigger hiring** `[05:03]` — alternative was leaving them unsubsidized and waiting. Reasoning: subsidy guarantees hiring momentum; subsidy can be removed after staffing kicks in and the building should keep hiring on its own.
- **Take Per Capita Taxation despite Kazoku/Trade Union opposition** `[08:03]` — alternative (the next-tier law) was rejected as "too much" because it removed peasant tax entirely. Reasoning: fulfils Goshu promise, introduces an income tax (none previously), shifts burden from peasants (huge population) to wage earners. Only legal government composition available; negotiation with trade unions only offered -5% stall chance, not worth it.
- **Build landing marines as a dedicated invasion army instead of using existing infantry** `[09:31]` — skirmish, mobile, artillery, shrapnel all carry -25% naval invasion efficiency; mechanized infantry was the only exception but unavailable. Marines cost 1.5/2.5 goods vs 1/2 but have same combat stats. Plan: ~20 marines as a permanent amphibious force.
- **Build marines in states with local supply of required goods** `[10:30]` — to keep input costs low.
- **Cancel some Promote Social Mobility decrees to free budget** `[11:02]` — rejected "City of Plenty" decree because failing to deliver promised goods generates radicals; better not to promise than to fail.
- **Pick offensive-trait commander (10% offense) for the marine army** `[13:30]` — accepted authoritarian ideology as a trade-off; relevant unit-combat traits noted (screening / admiral block / morale loss); offense is what matters for invading.
- **Decline Anti-Russian League foreign investment rights offer from Ottomans** `[17:31]` — flat no, no further analysis given.
- **Complete Emperor in Parliament journal entry** `[20:30]` — yields Responsible Government, replaces absolutists with royalists, avoids civil war. Royalists prefer monarchy over traditionalism; cleaner IG landscape.
- **Delay Per Capita enactment by 3 years** `[27:00]` — minus 4 modifier (acceptable), gives the marine training pool time to grow and reduces the naval-invasion penalty before the income shock.
- **Switch all mines to Nitroglycerin extraction method once unlocked** `[23:02]` — more iron output, more explosives throughput (50 -> 80 per level). Trade-off: more paper and fertilizer consumption, plus mortality. Iron mines prioritised since explosives demand was the binding constraint.
- **Build extra logging camps + iron mines + arms industries despite interest spike** `[24:35]` — bottlenecks (hardwood, iron, small arms) were pushing all military prices up. Accepted interest pain as cheaper than continued shortages.
- **Pursue Appointed Bureaucrats after Meiji succession** `[36:00]` — alternative was a law to please the radical movement (e.g. assembly rights), but ruled out giving up monarchy. Appointed Bureaucrats chosen over an institution-focused law because government-building taxation capacity was the more pressing economic issue, and it weakens aristocrats.
- **Accept political-concession negotiation: grant IG strength to leading group for +chance on Appointed Bureaucrats** `[37:02]` — preferred over a 24,000 money payment or cultural exclusion. Reasoning: IG strength to the favoured Goshu/modernizer faction compounds long-term.

## Reusable lessons

- **Admiral death drains fleet supply.** Always dock to recover rather than waiting at sea.
- **Subsidies are a hiring accelerator, not a permanent cost.** Subsidise a new building briefly to fill jobs, then remove the subsidy and let it run on its own.
- **Dedicated landing-marine corps beats reusing line troops for naval invasions.** Every non-mechanized infantry unit type carries -25% naval invasion efficiency; marines pay ~50% more goods upkeep but keep full landing efficiency and have identical combat stats.
- **Build military units in states with local supply** to reduce goods cost.
- **Cancel decrees you can't afford rather than failing promises.** Failed promises generate radicals; quiet cancellation is cheaper.
- **Pick commanders by trait fit, not ideology, when a role is specialised.** An offensive commander is the right pick for an invasion army even if their politics are inconvenient.
- **Resolve journal entries that replace IGs with friendlier ones (e.g. Responsible Government swaps absolutists for royalists).** Cheaper than fighting the IG landscape later.
- **Per Capita Taxation introduces an actual income tax** and shifts the burden from peasants to wage earners — net positive for Japan-shaped economies with huge peasant populations, even at the cost of Kazoku/Trade Union anger.
- **Delaying an enactment for a small modifier penalty is often worth it** when the in-flight cost (here: naval-invasion penalty from low marine training) is larger than the delay penalty.
- **Nitroglycerin PM on mines is a strict upgrade in throughput** (more iron, more explosives per level) at the cost of inputs (paper, fertilizer) and SoL/mortality — take it once you have the explosives pipeline.
- **Screening vulnerability ratio is just a targeting weight.** High-vulnerability ships (capitals) absorb more hits; low-vulnerability ships (cruisers) are skipped. You can flip the dynamic by building low-vulnerability high-attack cruisers as bait-inverters. Different from HoI4 screening.
- **Diagnose economic stress by checking input prices** (paper, construction goods, iron) plus government-buildings overhead. If construction inputs are expensive, pause building; if a strategic good is expensive, build extractors for it as priority.
- **Heir interest group matters** — a modernizer/Goshu heir compounds when they take the throne (here: +25% prominence to their IG on succession).
- **Sudden ruler death can reset your IG legitimacy** and force a re-think of which laws are passable. Expect to lose mid-enactment laws when the throne changes.
- **When negotiating an enactment, IG-strength concessions to a favoured group are usually better long-term than one-time cash** — they compound across future law attempts.
- **Power projection level 3 reduces infamy on conquest** — worth pushing fleet size for before declaring on weaker neighbours (here: Brunei, Johor).
- **Watch for expiring radical-suppression modifiers** (suffrage's -32 here). When the modifier drops, latent radicals surface; have a legitimate-government plan in place before the wave hits.
