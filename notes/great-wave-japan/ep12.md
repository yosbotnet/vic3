## Reusable lessons
- **Gov admin urbanization cheese**: Building level-1 government administrations is a fast, cheap way to push urban-center levels in a state to meet "X% of states have urban center level 5" journal requirements. Only 100 construction each, ~20 urbanization per level. Sell them off immediately after the journal completes because paper upkeep bleeds you dry.
- **Election meddling can flip an IG to opposition**: Using "favor party" during elections risks pushing the meddled IG out of government entirely if other IGs in your coalition object — leaving you with an illegitimate government and no path to enact laws. Don't meddle while mid-enactment of a controversial law.
- **Pause/cancel stalled enactments when legitimacy collapses**: A stalled law that's angering an IG locked out of government will block legitimacy recovery. Cancel it, restore the coalition, enact something safer (Appointed Bureaucrats was the recovery move here).
- **Securing the Power JE is character-targeted**: The "make person less prominent" action on the offending Kazoku character can close the journal in one click — much faster than waiting for natural clout decay.
- **Ships of the Line obsolete frigates immediately**: Once armor exceeds enemy hull attack, the lower-tier hull becomes useless in line battles. Only keep them for convoy raiding/protection if you have specific naval missions — otherwise full SotL.
- **Naval Administration has no goods upkeep**: Pure wage cost. Build wherever you have peasants + qualifications; placement doesn't matter for output. Note: bigger ships need bigger crews → higher wage bill not shown in template tooltip.
- **Emergency Relief is cheap insurance for capital + high-pop states only**: 20%/month chance of -5% devastation; authority is well-spent on key states, not worth spending on minor ones since devastation decays naturally.
- **Take the IG-pacifier option over short-term modifiers when planning law changes**: Events that offer "X IG less angry for 10 years" enable a whole law-change campaign window — often higher value than +education access or +authority for 5 years.
- **Fertilizer chain math**: 5 fertilizer plants needs ~150 sulfur and iron inputs (3× sulfur mines). Queue the input chain before the consumer to avoid input price spikes.
- **Tambora-equivalent volcanic event** (Eruption in Vestiyava ~1815-aligned in-game): Global devastation +9%, frost/hail/disease frequency up. Counter with Emergency Relief decree on top-priority states; accept the rest decays.
- **"Promised enactment" obligations from law negotiations** must be tracked — failing them affects future law passage. Sequence them before risky elections.
