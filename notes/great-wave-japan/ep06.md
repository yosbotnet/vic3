**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep6](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZL76TLF0hWg)

## Reusable lessons
- **Agendas with stochastic prerequisites (character-dependent IG clout) can be unwinnable from the start.** Recognize early and cut losses rather than burning opportunity cost. Author's own test runs: 2 wins / 2 losses — treat as 50/50, not a plan.
- **Naval meta at game start: build only ship-of-the-line with iron-reinforced hull.** Frigates can't damage SOLs at this tier; armor breakpoint (55) shuts down enemy medium guns. Maintenance/build cost premium is worth it.
- **Crewing is a separate problem from building.** Naval Administration is the bottleneck — queue it alongside (or before) shipyards or your fleet sits at low org.
- **Production Method upgrades that require a missing input are traps.** Don't switch PMs to steel tools, sulfur-based, or iron-frame variants until you confirm the supply chain or have the enabling tech inbound.
- **Time-limited research bonuses should go on whatever shortens the path to a pivotal tech**, not on whatever has the biggest raw number — permanent picks come later anyway.
- **Coup events: always pick the resistance/avoidance branch over offensive +coup-strength branches.** A successful military coup wipes law progress and locks the IG into government for ~20y. Asymmetric downside.
- **One steel mill flips PMs across an entire country's tooling chain.** You don't need parity production for steel before using steel tools; just need surplus.
- **Pre-commit construction sectors to higher-input PMs (iron-frame) just before the tech that cheapens the input lands.** Avoids a second switch cost.
- **Privatize aggressively by default** — frees treasury for construction, lets investment pool grow secondary industry.
- **Trade centers, not just ports, drive merchant marine for trade routes under open borders.** If trade-route capacity is short, build trade centers in major states.
- **When cashflow tips into a loan spiral, the order of operations is: throttle construction queue → switch off subsidies for buildings you aren't intentionally protecting → cut the least-politically-costly wage slider.** Don't try to fix it by enacting new laws.
- **Naval fleet missions scale with admiral rank** (regions covered) and **fleet size** (involvement generated). Project Power is the diplomatic-pressure mission; supply-ship draw grows with distance.
- **Tech priority for a struggling economy: currency standards → banking → central archives.** Banking reduces loan interest directly; central archives multiplies tax capacity and bureaucracy yield — both compound on cashflow stabilization.
- **Sunk-cost discipline on dead agendas pays off** — abandoning the Tenpo Crisis freed law focus for serfdom abolition the same session.
