# Episode 1 — Tokugawa setup: Tempo Crisis, locked country, and supply-ship priority

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MB0B8rY7YAc)

## What happened
Setup episode for a Japan run on the Great Wave DLC (1.13.0 pre-release). The player opens as the Tokugawa Shogunate under Sakoku and Closed Borders, immediately facing the Tempo Crisis journal entry (12-year timer) and a country-wide famine event ("Era of Heavenly Protection"). Most of the episode is spent surveying new DLC systems (ship construction, ship designer, Edo Status, national cast, involvement/interest mechanics) and committing to a reformer-leaning agenda aimed at restoring the Emperor while building a navy from scratch.

## Decisions made
- **Target the reformer side of the Tempo Crisis** rather than hardliner [03:31][04:31]. Reasoning: wants the Emperor restored over the Shogunate and the Chonin (petite bourgeoisie) buffed; the reformer completion bonus gives the Chonin more traction, which aligns with the broader goal of opening the country.
- **Click "combat rice hoarding" agenda goal** as soon as the grain-price threshold is met [05:00][34:00]. Reasoning: removes the +10% peasant goods consumption modifier, which is huge given Japan starts pop-heavy in peasants and is in active famine.
- **Pursue the "Edo–Osaka confiscation order" agenda** by building Naval Fortifications in Kanto and Osaka [05:30][06:01]. Reasoning: cheap building, doubles as anti-naval-invasion defence, gives more tax collection (Japan starts with very poor collection) and reduces decree cost there. Also feeds the "10+ Naval Fortifications" Tempo objective.
- **Skip the "Chonin threaten stability" click** [05:00]. Reasoning: it weakens the Chonin via a Loosened Status modifier; the player wants the Chonin stronger, not weaker.
- **Spend authority on Promote Social Mobility in every state** [08:02][09:30]. Reasoning: even after the devs halved the qualifications penalty from the Edo class system, qualifications are still poor; the decree also grants education access. Remaining authority kept for consumption taxes on liquor/tobacco.
- **Research order: Cotton Gin → Lathe → Atmospheric Engine, then jump to next propulsion tech** [10:30][17:02]. Reasoning: production/construction tech still beats the new DLC techs for an early backwards economy; Atmospheric Engine is the gate to iron-frame buildings and to viable engine-equipped ships.
- **Keep construction sectors on wooden buildings for now** [11:32]. Reasoning: not enough iron; switch to iron-frame only after Atmospheric Engine.
- **Slider shipyards 100% to supply ships at start** [12:31][13:30][22:00]. Reasoning: the starting Merchant Navy law grants +30% supply-ship construction efficiency, and military ship templates require artillery/iron the player does not yet produce. Supply ships are also the new replacement for convoys.
- **Defer building any military ships until Atmospheric Engine + an artillery economy exist** [15:31][17:02]. Reasoning: ship cost scales exponentially with each module; cheapest viable ship-of-the-line still needs 3 artillery and 5 artillery / 3 iron for guns, which Japan can't supply.
- **Improve relations with Ryukyu, Ezochi, and the Netherlands** [17:31][18:01]. Reasoning: lock in the vassals; the Netherlands action makes the Intelligentsia happier (political lobby) and incidentally generates Involvement in the Dutch region under the new mechanic.
- **Try to push Chonin above 5% so they qualify for government** [07:32][29:01]. Reasoning: only the Chonin and Rangakusha want the bureaucracy and Lex Status changes that complete the reformer Tempo goals; with the daimyo dominant, the cabinet currently can change nothing.
- **Swap Local Police → Dedicated Police Force as a "do something" first law change** [31:30][32:31]. Reasoning: success chance is low (28% × 0.5), but the daimyo aren't actively opposed, and it's a way to start moving the country. Negotiates with the Devout (very collaborative) using political concessions to lift success chance to ~14%.
- **For the famine "For a Bowl of Rice" event, pay village headsmen to quell unrest** [35:33]. Reasoning: the Rural Folk are in government so the option is available; chosen over letting it burn to limit devastation and pop loss.
- **Wait for an event to break Closed Borders rather than forcing it now** [36:00]. Reasoning: three paths exist (lawful reform, great-power forced opening, scripted event); the lawful path is blocked because no political movement yet wants opening, so the event is the cheapest exit.

## Outcomes
- Combat-rice-hoarding agenda completed in-episode [34:00], removing the peasant consumption penalty.
- Promote Social Mobility decree placed in every state, exhausting authority as planned.
- Shipyards queued fully on supply ships; military shipbuilding intentionally deferred.
- Dedicated Police Force law change started at ~14% chance after Devout negotiation — outcome not visible this episode.
- "Era of Heavenly Protection" triggered immediately, confirming the Tempo Crisis is on a hard timer and forcing famine management on top of reform work.
- Chonin still stuck below 5% at episode end — none of the reform agenda goals that require them in government can be attempted yet.
- ~16.6M pops in mild starvation at episode start; no quick fix available, only structural (build rice farms, finish Tempo).

## Reusable lessons
- **When the starting cabinet is locked by one dominant interest group, accept that the first 12-year window is mostly about repositioning, not legislating.** Identify the marginalized group whose agenda you actually want (here: Chonin), and spend authority/characters/laws on raising their clout rather than burning attempts on doomed law swaps.
- **Use the "marginalized group must reach 5% before they can enter cabinet" threshold as a planning gate.** Until they cross it, the reformer-side journal goals are unreachable; treat hitting that 5% as the real first objective behind the visible journal entry.
- **Bias early authority toward decrees that unblock qualifications (Promote Social Mobility) when starting under a restrictive class/caste law.** Even after dev nerfs, qualification penalties from systems like Edo Status will silently strangle hiring across the whole economy.
- **Clear consumption-penalty modifiers (e.g. rice hoarding) before chasing GDP goals.** A flat % goods-consumption hit on your largest pop stratum is worth more SoL/stability than several new buildings.
- **Sequence shipbuilding behind the Atmospheric Engine and a domestic artillery/iron base.** Ship module costs scale exponentially, and the cheapest viable hull still needs goods a pre-industrial economy doesn't make. Until then, slider shipyards to whatever your current naval law subsidises (supply ships under Merchant Navy here).
- **Treat supply ships as a strategic resource, not a logistics afterthought, under the new ship system.** They replace convoys, gate fleet endurance, and can be mass-produced cheaply while you wait for the tech and goods that real warships need.
- **Naval Fortifications are a double-dip building early game**: they satisfy journal/agenda objectives, blunt naval invasions, and are cheap/fast to put down — prioritise them over more glamorous military buildings when an agenda asks for them.
- **Don't blow authority cheesing characters/generals the way you might have in older patches.** Promoting low-strata characters into ranks to flip clout now triggers approval penalties; influence interest groups through patronage/peerage of existing characters instead.
- **When a law has 0% success chance, find the most collaborative opposing IG and bribe with the cheapest concession** (political concessions over money when the IG only cares about strength). Even ~14% lets you start chipping; doing nothing guarantees zero progress.
- **For locked-country starts, plan three parallel exit paths** (lawful reform, great-power gunboat opening, scripted event) and don't commit to the lawful path until at least one IG movement actually exists that wants opening. Otherwise you're spending political capital against a wall.
- **Reformer vs hardliner Tempo-style journal entries reward asymmetric completion** — partial completion on each side gives a different bonus than going all-in on one. Decide which bonus matters for your long-game plan before picking goals, not after.
