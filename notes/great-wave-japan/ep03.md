# Episode 3 — Picking the Lock: Ending Sakoku and Annexing Ryukyu

**Source:** [Victoria 3: Japan Becomes a Superpower! | The Great Wave - ep3](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tDYzKbXgR4E)

## What happened
The Miroslavski incident fires, giving Japan the option to end Sakoku. The player takes it, immediately revokes Sakoku, and switches the trade law to Mercantilism, opening the market and unlocking a cascade of new journal entries: Earning Recognition, the Hidden Domain (Ryukyu), and the Honorable Restoration (Sonnō jōi). The player wins the Ryukyu sway contest against Qing via a Military Access Treaty plus mutual obligations, then turns to industrial setup (lathes, dye workshops, lead glass) and improving relations with great powers ahead of recognition.

## Reusable lessons
- **Time-limited "opening" modifiers are launch windows, not buffs to hoard.** When a one-shot modifier like Picking the Lock gives a 10-year clout boost to your reform IGs, fire it as early as possible so the entire window overlaps your law-change queue.
- **Compare malus duration to your strategic horizon before flinching at big negative numbers.** A -67% IG approval that decays in 5 years is cheaper than a permanent -44% blocker if you're playing an 80-year game.
- **For sway-contest journal entries, find the cheapest interaction unlock.** Don't build expensive infrastructure (ports, shipyards) in the contested state if a diplomatic pact (Military Access, Money Transfer, Obligations) yields the same monthly sway tick. Stack multiple low-cost pacts rather than one big build.
- **Max the cash on obligation/transfer deals.** The deal-points formula rewards the maximum transferable amount; partial offers waste the diplomatic slot.
- **Pre-route around ruler ideology locks.** If a critical reform button gates on ruler ideology and both ruler + heir are wrong, plan an alternative path (slow clout grind, different journal) instead of waiting on the succession RNG.
- **Don't trigger high-risk movement events at low legitimacy.** "Unforeseen consequences" warnings on petition-type interactions are a hard signal to defer until you have a fallback (legitimacy buffer or civil-war-capable army).
- **New tech = audit every PM it touches.** When a tech unlocks PMs (Lathes → dyes, lead glass, furniture-with-tools), walk the whole production chain: unlocked PM, building that consumes the new output (Urban Centre PM for glass), and inputs you already produce locally. Free GDP is usually sitting one click away.
- **Fix input-price spikes at the source state.** Building a Tooling Workshop / Iron Mine in the state doing the construction is cheaper than freight-importing; local-market prices dominate construction cost.
- **Start the great-power relation grind before you're eligible.** Recognition / diplomatic journals usually need 50 relations with a GP; relations climb slowly, so begin improving while still law-locked from progress.
- **Don't promote IGs whose ideology contradicts your plan, even when they're convenient.** Recruiting Generals from a Samurai-style backward IG buys short-term army strength but adds permanent clout to a faction blocking your reforms — usually a bad trade.
- **Bureaucracy regularly goes negative right after enacting a new institution.** Pre-queue Urban Centres in your lowest-taxation incorporated state before passing institution laws, not after.
- **Watch institution-cap stacking sources.** Max institution level is the sum of country + tech + centralisation modifiers; check the breakdown before promising yourself a plan that needs level 3.
