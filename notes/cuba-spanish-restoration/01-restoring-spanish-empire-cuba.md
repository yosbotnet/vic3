# Cuba — Restoring the Spanish Empire (Tarkusarkusar, single video)

**Source:** [Restoring the SPANISH EMPIRE as CUBA - Victoria 3 GIGACHAD STORY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rKZwT9G7Dq8) by Tarkusarkusar, 2025-09-04, 28:32. Patch unspecified (presumed 1.10–1.11 era).

## What happened
Single-video Cuba run that goes from 1836 Spanish colony to ~1905 socialist republic. Opens as a plantation island under Miguel Tacón (cruel trait), pumps Spanish migration with low taxes and a plantation economy, signs an obligation-for-independence-support treaty with the US, fights a Dominion war in 1849–1851 to gain diplomatic freedom while keeping Spanish market access, then runs a 15-year reconquista across Central America and Northern South America (Honduras → Nicaragua → Costa Rica → Venezuela → Haiti → New Granada). Independence from Spain follows in 1867 and Great Power status the year after. The mid-game stalls into a budget crisis around 1876 caused by an investment pool too small for the construction queue — diagnosed correctly as a profits problem, not a spending problem. The fix is "coffee colonialism": invading Zulu, Mozambique, Benin, and Sokoto and leaving them unincorporated so that low-wage plantation profits flood the investment pool. Two attempts to attack Britain both fail (one save-reloaded, one accepted as the ending) because Cuba on Market Capitalism is structurally vulnerable to convoy raiding. The run closes on a Council Republic transition (Fidel Laredo, March 1899) and lands ~100M GDP with a balanced budget and a power bloc that resembles, if not actually restores, the old Spanish Empire. The video is openly an unoptimized teaching run — failures are narrated rather than cut.

## Decisions made
- **Build only coffee plantations early, not industry or any other plantation good** [02:05][02:35]. Reasoning: of Cuba's plantation goods, only coffee has a prestige-goods company; thematic tobacco/cigars has no company prerequisite. Plantations are also 200 CP for ~5K jobs vs 800 CP / 5K jobs for a steel mill, so four plantations finish in the time of one steel mill — 4× the pops, 4× the SoL lift, 4× the migration attraction. The early-game lever is pops created per construction-week, not profit per building.
- **Defer building the first construction sector until the budget can afford 2–3 at once** [03:36][04:06]. Reasoning: one construction sector takes base CP from 10 → 15, but the private sector immediately claims half, leaving 7.5 state CP. With nothing yet for the investment pool to expand into, that 7.5 of IP is wasted and the player is net-down from the original 10. Wait until plantations are profitable enough that the IP allocation has somewhere productive to go, then build 3 sectors in one batch.
- **Drop taxes to minimum to pump Spanish migration** [04:06]. Reasoning: as a colony Cuba's main pop pipeline is Spanish migrants, who only come if SoL is competitive. The "lost" tax revenue is bought back in pop count, IG strength, and later tax-base size; an obligation-for-cash treaty with the US covers the gap in the meantime.
- **Sign Cuban Freedom Congress with the US (obligation in exchange for independence support, money transfer, military access)** [04:06]. Reasoning: a belligerent target's enemy is the cheapest source of independence-support diplomatic action; the £700/month transfer also funds the migration-friendly low-tax posture. Later renegotiated to Cuban Liberation Congress at a higher payout after Spain dragged Cuba into a war that broke the first treaty [07:40].
- **Build 3 construction sectors at once in 1839** [04:37]. Reasoning: with three sectors the state-side share is 12.5 CP (vs the original 10), the IP side is also 12.5, and at that point there's enough productive industry — coffee plantations and the developing tool chain — for the IP to feed without waste. The "single sector is net-negative" rule inverts at 2–3 sectors.
- **Pause construction during 1846–1848 budget crunch** [07:40][08:12]. Reasoning: with no railways yet, the infrastructure cap was throttling the investment pool's contribution and the government was funding everything alone. Pausing the queue stops the bleed while railway research completes; resumes once one railway opens the cap.
- **Fight for Dominion, not independence, in 1849** [08:42]. Reasoning: Dominion gains independent diplomacy and freedom from forced participation in overlord wars without losing Spanish market access (still valuable as a coffee exporter) and without paying the unrecognized-power interest-rate penalty. Independence is sequenced later, after great-power readiness.
- **Two parallel naval landings in Haiti during the Dominion war** [09:13]. Reasoning: a single landing would have to fight through the combined Spanish + Haitian armies. Splitting into a second landing on the eastern half of the island shortcuts that grind by occupying war goals from two directions.
- **Cuban Metal as the second company pick** [10:44]. Reasoning: basic metal company increases iron-mine throughput, which lowers the price line on construction goods. The saving on the build queue is worth more than the building's own profit shows up as.
- **Brute-force the Venezuelan war against Brazil's intervention via enlistment decree + high military wages** [11:14]. Reasoning: Cuba was outgunned on land but had naval dominance, which meant battalions could be reinforced faster on the front than Brazil could. The combination forced a stalemate Cuba could outlast.
- **Annex Haiti directly after Venezuela** [11:44]. Reasoning: Haiti was a Spanish puppet inherited as Cuba's subject; liberty desire bookkeeping on a subject too small to protect is an attention tax. Cleaning up between major wars was cheaper than leaving it for later.
- **Add humiliation alongside the independence wargoal vs Spain (July 1866)** [13:15]. Reasoning: humiliation might bait Spain into a costlier defence and reduce its willingness to drag the war out. The actual war ended in one year because Spain over-committed an army to Mexico that the US destroyed, collapsing Spanish war support.
- **Take Wealth Voting before enacting Monarchy** [14:17][15:48]. Reasoning: voting law gates which IG leaders are candidates for emperor; Wealth Voting opens the candidate pool wider than the default landowner-dominated field. The election that resolves the monarchy law also picks the emperor, so the sequencing matters.
- **Form Power Bloc Novomundo as Sovereign Empire** [18:20]. Reasoning: Sovereign Empire archetype requires no vassals, just sufficient influence. The Novomundo framing makes the reconquista narratively coherent without committing to a specific membership template.
- **Save-reload the first Britain war** [19:21][19:52]. Reasoning: under Market Capitalism every internal trade route counts as a convoy route; Britain's convoy raiding cut Atlantic *and* in-empire market access (Cuba ↔ Panama, Cuba ↔ African plantations) within a few months. The war was unrecoverable on the original save.
- **Invade Mexico as consolation** [19:52]. Reasoning: weakening a Britain-aligned mid-power partly offsets the failed war and gives an easy victory while the real strategy is reformulated. Stress-relief move; in narration the player calls it "pretty pointless."
- **Invade Zulu / Portuguese Mozambique / Benin / Sokoto for unincorporated arable land** [20:53][21:23]. Reasoning: coffee plantations on unincorporated discriminated land have collapsed wages and explosive profits; those profits feed the investment pool, which then pays for the construction queue the state can't. Violence suppression and road maintenance decrees accelerate the build queue while the conquest is fresh.
- **Tariff own coffee exports** [22:55]. Reasoning: budget was the priority over GDP. The tariff raised ~£60K/month income but the home trade center on tariffed routes lost ~£44K/month and dropped volume, so part of the tariff income comes out of the player's own export GDP. Acceptable when treasury > GDP in the priority stack.
- **Release a colonial administration over the African plantation states** [23:26]. Reasoning: a colonial administration immediately incorporates the states it's given (so taxes and tariffs now work) and remits tribute to the overlord. Trades direct control for tax efficiency; timed after the unincorporated wage-suppression has done most of its work building the plantations.
- **Pick Commercial Agriculture as the Council Republic compromise, not Collectivized Farming** [26:00]. Reasoning: Cuba's farming sector was small (plantations dominate, not farms), so collectivizing it would be a marginal economic gain at the cost of upsetting the rich-moderate-radical balance needed to pass cooperative ownership. Match the compromise law to the size of the sector it modifies.
- **Pick the communist branch on Workers Republic, not anarchism** [26:31]. Reasoning: anarchism's only useful reward is multiculturalism; the other rewards are "total trash" in the player's words. Pick by mechanical reward unless roleplaying.

## Outcomes
- Migration pump worked: ~half a million Spanish pops in Cuba by mid-1847.
- Dominion war (Nov 1849 – Apr 1851) won; Cuba gained own diplomacy while staying in Spanish market.
- Reconquista: Honduras, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Venezuela (vs Brazil), Haiti, and most of New Granada annexed between 1851 and 1867.
- Spain surrendered September 1867; Cuba independent.
- Great Power status January 1868; national debt cleared by summer 1870.
- Mid-game budget crisis 1876: too much construction for what the investment pool could fund. Correctly diagnosed as a profits problem, not a spending problem.
- Power Bloc Novomundo formed 1880 as Sovereign Empire archetype with zero vassals.
- First Britain war (1880s): lost to convoy raiding under Market Capitalism, save-reloaded.
- Coffee colonialism (Zulu/Mozambique/Benin/Sokoto) repaired the budget: from 50M GDP deficit in 1884 to balanced budget at ~95M GDP by December 1891.
- Council Republic enacted March 1899 (Fidel Laredo); Commercial Agriculture passed June 1903; Cooperative Ownership passed September 1904.
- Second Britain war (July 1905): the US joined Britain this time, war clearly unwinnable; run accepted the defeat as the ending.
- Final state: ~100M GDP, balanced budget, council republic, power bloc Novomundo, no actual Spanish-empire formation but a thematically resonant Hispanic-Atlantic union.

## Reusable lessons
- **Pop pump first, profits second.** Early game, the lever is pops created per construction-week, not profit per building. Plantations beat industry on this lever because they're cheap to build and employ many. Industry only beats plantations once SoL, migration, and the prestige-goods company chain have all matured.
- **Plantation choice should be selected by available prestige-goods company, not raw price.** The endgame company multiplier is bigger than any short-term price advantage; pick the crop your eventual company will be able to consume.
- **Construction sectors have an inflection point: one is worse than zero, three is better than one.** A lone sector activates the private/state CP split with nothing for the private side to do, so the state ends up with less usable CP than before. Three sectors flip the math because the IP has enough share to feed something useful and the state share is also larger in absolute terms.
- **Out-of-infrastructure halts the investment pool, not the state queue.** A sudden mid-build budget collapse usually means the IP went idle at the infrastructure cap and the state is now paying for everything alone. The fix is railways, not less construction.
- **Dominion is the cheap intermediate between subject and independent.** It buys independent diplomacy and freedom from forced wars while preserving market access and dodging the unrecognized-power interest penalty. Use it when independence proper would cost market access or trigger interest-rate pain.
- **Treaty rupture is an upside.** When an overlord drags you into a war that breaks a foreign treaty, the renegotiated version typically pays better. Don't mourn the broken pact — re-sign at the higher number.
- **The mid-game budget crisis is structural, not tactical.** A construction-heavy nation with no profitable industry feeding its investment pool will burn through the treasury. The diagnostic isn't "am I overspending" but "is my IP big enough to pay its share of the construction queue." If not, the fix is more profitable industry, not less construction.
- **Coffee colonialism is the IP-repair move.** Conquer or colonize low-wage land, leave it unincorporated, build prestige-goods-company plantations there. Wage collapse → profit explosion → IP swells → state stops paying for construction. Violence suppression + road maintenance decrees accelerate the build queue.
- **Colonial administration release converts unincorporated control into tribute and tax efficiency.** Releasing a colonial admin over your plantation states immediately incorporates them (so you can tax/tariff) and remits tribute. Time it after the unincorporated wage-suppression has finished doing useful work.
- **Tariffs are a budget knob, not a growth knob.** A tariff on your own export costs the home trade center on that route, which then drops volume; part of the tariff income comes out of your own GDP. Use them when treasury > GDP in your priority stack.
- **Market Capitalism makes you structurally vulnerable to convoy raiding by a major navy.** Internal market access between non-adjacent states travels by convoy under this law, so an enemy who can sink your convoys severs your domestic economy as well as your overseas one. Don't pick a fight with the world's biggest navy on this law without a continental land base.
- **Re-check alliance composition before each major war.** Allies who joined your side once are not committed to joining again. Lifecycle matters: a 30-year-old alliance may be politically inert by the time you call in the favor.
- **Match a compromise law to the size of the sector it modifies.** Collectivizing 5% of an economy isn't worth the political cost; commercial agriculture as the agrarian compromise was correct because Cuba's farming was a small share of the economy. Choose by sector size, not by ideological purity.
