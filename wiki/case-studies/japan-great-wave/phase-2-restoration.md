# Phase 2 — The Honorable Restoration scramble

**Episodes:** [ep04](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep04.md) · [ep05](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep05.md) · [ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md) · [ep07](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep07.md) · [ep08](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep08.md)

**TL;DR:** Japan races two overlapping timers — the still-ticking Tempo Crisis and the new Honorable Restoration agenda — concedes Tempo to focus on serfdom abolition, then manually engineers the fall of the Tokugawa by spiking Sonno Joi activism and save-scumming the Restoration button under low shogun legitimacy. The phase ends with the Empire of Japan formed, the daimyo converted into the Kazoku, and three 12-year journal-entry clocks (Mission to the West, Industrialize Japan, Retire the Samurai) all started simultaneously.

## Setting the stage

Phase 1 ended with Sakoku revoked, the "Picking the Lock" launch window burning, Ryukyu annexed, and the Rangakusha morphed into the Intelligentsia. The Tempo Crisis is still running with a hard timer, and the new Honorable Restoration shared agenda has begun. Japan is structurally healthier — construction cost has dropped, supply ships are flowing — but the political problem is unchanged: the cabinet remains Daimyo-dominated, the petite bourgeoisie (Chonin) clout sits near 9% against a 10% target, and almost no law clears with a survivable stall chance without active manipulation.

The phase's targets are concrete and stacked. The reformer side of the Tempo Crisis demands 8 of 10 reform events. Honorable Restoration demands Capital Fleets, 10 Naval Fortifications, GDP > 14.3, and a path to Imperial Restoration (either Sonno Joi > 50% activism or a non-Traditionalist shogun). Behind those sits the strategic prize: the Empire of Japan, an end to the Daimyo's institutional veto, and the three Restoration journal entries that gate the rest of the campaign. The constraint is the calendar — every event is on a clock, and the navy is busy building supply ships rather than transports, so foreign adventures are off the table.

## Narrative

The phase opens with a clean optimization run on the Honorable Restoration checklist. Capital Fleets is enacted off a free petite-bourgeoisie petition — the Devout extract a costly Standard of Living 12 commitment to clear the law, which the player flags in-episode as a probable mistake and which immediately spawns a Traditionalist Catholic agitator. Naval Fortifications are stood up in Kanto and Osaka to unlock the Edo–Osaka Confiscation Order, which fires the 10-Re Consolidation Act: decree cost collapses to ~40 in the two richest agriculture states, and a Farming Boom event pushes Osaka throughput to ~46%. The shogun's heir reaches adulthood as a Moderate, opening a second, parallel path to Imperial Restoration that doesn't require Sonno Joi to break 50%.

By [ep05](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep05.md) the agenda checklist is mostly cleared — Capital Fleets adopted, 10 fortifications built, GDP across 14.3, ships of the line on the slipways with atmospheric engines — but Chonin clout refuses to move. Peerage grants pull magnates into the National Cabinet, Furniture and Textile Mills are queued to grow Shopkeeper pops, and Hereditary Bureaucrats removal is deliberately paused until the -1 Daimyo modifier expires. The Shogun is openly worried that the run will fail the agenda inside the remaining ~2y 10m window. A backup path is scouted in case the primary fails: deliberately provoke an IG over 27.5% to crisis-enact Rigid Status Order.

The Tempo Crisis dies in [ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md). Chonin clout collapses from ~10 to ~7 with no recoverable path, and the player formally concedes at 6/8, redirecting the freed law slot to Tenant Farmers. The same episode survives a samurai coup attempt by Brig. Gen. Noki via a church decision granting +15 coup resistance (chosen over a +coup-strength alternative). The naval doctrine is locked in — ship-of-the-line only, iron-reinforced hull, 55 armor — because frigates can't damage SOLs and 55 armor shuts down enemy medium guns. Naval Administration is built to crew the fleet, the steel mill comes online and Bessemer + steel tools propagate across the tooling chain, and construction sectors are pre-committed to Iron-Frame Buildings ahead of the water-tube boiler tech. Emperor Ninko dies; the 15-year-old, sickly, samurai-aligned Komei succeeds.

[ep07](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep07.md) is the engineered restoration. Tenant Farmers passes after a deliberately-used setback raises the next attempt's chance. Sonno Joi activism is then driven across 50% on purpose — Rigid Edo Status is enacted because the movement opposes it, and 5-year Trade Privileges treaties are handed out to Renai, Bengal, and other minors because each one further enrages the xenophobic faction. The Imperial Restoration button is then save-scummed: the first click, with shogun legitimacy still high, produces Taisei Hokan (the emperor reaffirms the shogun — the wrong branch). Reload, mass-anger interest groups to tank legitimacy, click again — this time the Decree of Restoration fires. The Tokugawa fall, Japan becomes the Empire of Japan, and the indefinite Bakumatsu modifier (+500% political activism) begins. In parallel, treaty upkeep with Britain and the Netherlands is cancelled to stop the 26% unrecognized-power interest from destroying the budget, paper mills go up across Kanto and Osaka to crush institution input prices, and the vassal Ezoki is annexed and incorporated.

The Komei Restoration completes one month later in [ep08](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep08.md). The Imperial faction wins a Sonno Joi political debate — save-scummed at high success-chance ticks to avoid an uprising — and the Bakufu falls cleanly. The capital relocates to Kanto, the daimyo convert into the Kazoku (losing Bakuhan, gaining Hierarchic), and three 12-year journal entries open at once: Dispatch a Mission to the West, Industrialize Japan, Retire the Samurai. Maeda Yoshiyasu (Chonin leader, sick but ambitious) is sent on the Iwakura-equivalent tour: short stops in Mexico, long stays in Britain and France, then Prussia, the Balkans, the Ottomans, Greece, and Ceylon. He returns as a masterful diplomat / explorer / modernizer, and the mission report picks Public Schools over a flat +6% research speed bonus. Frontier Colonization passes — chosen ahead of Agrarianism because it doesn't radicalize anyone — and Shinbutsu Bunri is triggered for the journal stack. A second coup attempt is defused without combat by simply unassigning the rebelling Lieutenant General, who then ticks at -14%/mo. The phase closes with Recognition tracking automatically (serfdom abolished, ≥50 relation with a great power), Industrialize Japan on track but tight, and the Kazoku/samurai approval tanked from foreign investment + Shinbutsu Bunri — the bill for the restoration coming due.

## Key decisions

- **Take the reformer side of the Tempo Crisis even though it will fail.** The reformer agenda grants +20% Chonin pop attraction; the hardliner side buffs the Samurai. The same Chonin work also feeds the Honorable Restoration's petite-bourgeoisie-clout requirement, so the direction of effort is correct even after Tempo is conceded at 6/8 [[ep04](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep04.md), [ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md)].

- **Enact Capital Fleets immediately on a free petite-bourgeoisie petition, but eat the Devout's SoL-12 concession to clear it.** The alternative concessions (-30% bureaucracy for 10 years, or 40% IG strength to the Devout under the new law) were worse on paper, but the player flagged the SoL-12 pick as a probable mistake in-episode: Standard of Living 12 was not going to be hit inside the modifier's lifetime, leaving the Devout huge attraction for years and spawning a Traditionalist Catholic agitator [[ep04](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep04.md)].

- **Build Naval Fortifications in Kanto and Osaka to unlock the Edo–Osaka Confiscation Order.** The Imperious ruler trait gives -20% decree cost; the resulting 10-Re Consolidation Act adds another decree-cost reducer plus +30% tax collection for 20 years in Japan's two richest agriculture states. Decree cost in those states drops to ~40, at which point cheap agricultural decrees become efficient throughput buffs. On the "Landholders Resist Centralization" event, the player picked "Remind them this is the will of the Shogun" over the cheaper Offer Compensation — because compensating buffed daimyo strength by +15%, which would have undone the entire centralization point [[ep04](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep04.md)].

- **Pause action and wait out the -1 Daimyo modifier before pushing Hereditary Bureaucrats removal.** Reading the Modifiers tab showed ~16 months remaining, leaving ~3 years inside the agenda window to enact. The alternative — push immediately — meant eating a high stall chance against an angry IG. The wait dropped the eventual stall chance to 15% [[ep05](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep05.md)].

- **Grant Peerage to Petite Bourgeoisie magnates and build Furniture / Textile Mills to grow Shopkeeper pops.** Once magnates max prominence and own all available holdings, the only remaining levers on Chonin clout are moving PB characters into noble-eligible cabinet slots, and growing the underlying pop. Furniture employs 1000 Shopkeepers per level — the highest of any building checked. The lever ultimately failed (Chonin crept 9% → ~9%) but it was the correct play given the constraints [[ep05](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep05.md)].

- **Concede the Tempo Crisis at 6/8 and redirect the freed law slot to Tenant Farmers.** Chonin clout had collapsed from ~10 to ~7 with no recoverable path inside the remaining window. The alternative was burning more law/agenda attempts on a lost cause; the freed slot went straight into ending serfdom via the bolstered Peasant Movement [[ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md)].

- **Pick the church's +15 coup resistance over the +10 coup strength branch when a samurai coup fires.** Asymmetric downside: a successful military coup wipes all law progress and locks the rebelling IG into government for ~22 years. The resistance branch costs -5 legitimacy, -bureaucracy, -authority for 2 years — acceptable insurance [[ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md)].

- **Build only Ships of the Line with iron-reinforced hull (55 armor) at this tech tier.** Frigates can't damage SOLs at this tier; 55 armor shuts down enemy SOLs with medium guns (55 attack) — neither side penetrates. The ~2x maintenance and slower build is cheaper than 4–5 frigates and far more effective. Naval Administration is queued alongside shipyards because crewing is a separate problem — without 900 sailors / 100 officers / 1000 servicemen per level, the hulls sit at low organization [[ep05](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep05.md), [ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md)].

- **Deliberately spike Sonno Joi activism above 50% by enacting laws it opposes.** Rigid Edo Status was enacted not for its own merits but because the xenophobic movement opposed it; 5-year Trade Privileges treaties were handed out to Renai, Bengal, and other minors as further xenophobe-activism levers. Movement activism is a tool, not a side effect — it's the gate for the Imperial Restoration petition button [[ep07](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep07.md)].

- **Save-scum the Imperial Restoration button to find the correct legitimacy state.** First click, under high shogun legitimacy, produced Taisei Hokan (emperor reaffirms shogun — the wrong branch). Reload, mass-anger interest groups to tank legitimacy, then click again — produced the Decree of Restoration that abolishes the shogunate. The "unforeseen consequences" warning on low legitimacy is the *desired* path for the restoration arc [[ep07](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep07.md)].

- **Cancel discretionary upkeep before cutting construction when interest spirals.** As an unrecognized power on 26% interest, the order of operations was: cancel trade-privilege treaties (Britain, Netherlands), refuse to subsidize unprofitable ports, privatize coal mines and other state-owned buildings, cut government wages (least politically costly slider). Construction stayed mostly running [[ep07](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep07.md), [ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md)].

- **Komei Restoration: pick Intelligentsia (then flip to Industrialist Gosho 50%) over the default ally.** The +50% IG strength matters less than aligning the ally's law preferences with the endgame target laws — Intelligentsia wants appointed bureaucrats, private schools, no slavery, constitutional monarchy. The player initially picked Intelligentsia for ideological alignment, then took the Gosho 50% to push industrialists through faster — both moves reasoned about law preferences rather than headline boost [[ep08](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep08.md)].

- **Send Maeda Yoshiyasu (Chonin, sick, ambitious) on the Mission to the West.** All candidates were sick, so prominence and health were sunk inputs; the choice criteria were trait synergy with the mission reward (returns popular, ideology shifts toward visited nations' laws — the modernizer outcome). Mexico got a short stop; Britain and France long stays because they're the templates for industrial and political modernization. The mission report picked Public Schools over a one-off +6% research speed bonus — institutions compound, modifiers don't [[ep08](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep08.md)].

- **Defuse the second coup attempt by unassigning the rebelling Lieutenant General.** No command means no coup progress — the meter ticks at -14%/mo and dies without combat. Generalised lesson noted in-episode: "don't make your armies too big" — keep field armies split so no single commander has decisive force [[ep08](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep08.md)].

## Phase-specific lessons

- **Movement activism is a deliberate input, not a side effect.** You can intentionally enact laws a movement opposes to spike its activism toward a journal-entry threshold. Trade Privileges treaties to minors are a reliable xenophobe-activism lever; rigid status laws are a reliable liberal/peasant lever. The "punishment" for a hostile movement is also the "fuel" for its journal-entry button. See ../../laws-and-politics/political-movements.md.

- **Branched buttons whose outcome depends on hidden state are save-scummable.** The same Imperial Restoration click produces Taisei Hokan or Decree of Restoration based on shogun legitimacy at the moment of click. Save before pressing, test the branch, reload to the correct precondition, then click. Reading the warning text matters — "unforeseen consequences" on low legitimacy is the *desired* path here.

- **Sunk-cost discipline on dead agendas frees a law slot the same session.** Tempo Crisis math was unrecoverable by [ep06](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep06.md); cutting it the moment that became visible allowed Tenant Farmers to start immediately. The author's own test runs are 2 wins / 2 losses on Tempo — treat character-dependent IG-clout agendas as 50/50, not a plan, and always scout a backup trigger (here: rigid status order via crisis path).

- **Never commit to a Standard-of-Living target you can't hit inside the modifier's lifetime.** The Capital Fleets SoL-12 Devout concession spawned a Traditionalist Catholic agitator and kept Devout attraction high for years — a cheaper concession would have been net positive. The headline +% boost on a commitment is meaningless if the target won't be met. See ../../pops/standard-of-living.md.

- **Crewing is a separate problem from building.** Naval Administration (900 sailors / 100 officers / 1000 servicemen per level) is the bottleneck — queue it alongside or before shipyards or the fleet sits at low organization. Shipyards themselves have no input-shortage penalty, so hulls can be mass-produced before the artillery / iron supply chain is ready. See ../../military/navy.md.

- **Run the journal-entry clock and the law-change clock in parallel from day one.** Three 12-year Restoration journals (Mission to the West, Industrialize Japan, Retire the Samurai) open simultaneously on Komei Restoration completion — they assume the player is already enacting, building, and researching the prerequisites. Sequential play misses the window. Restoration / unique national journals also have implicit law prerequisites not obvious from the journal text — pre-check and chain them.

## What to take into Phase 3

The Empire of Japan exists, but it sits on an indefinite Bakumatsu modifier (+500% political activism), a Kazoku-dominated cabinet that won't pass Lex Edo Status, and 26% loan interest until Recognition lands. Phase 3 is the recognition grind, the constitutional pivot through Landed Voting, and the Government-Administration spam that satisfies Industrialize Japan inside its 12-year clock.
