# Phase 4 — Economy under stress, Korea aborted

**Episodes:** [ep13](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep13.md) · [ep14](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep14.md) · [ep15](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep15.md)

**TL;DR:** Japan exits the recognition grind into a treasury squeeze — paper and iron eat the government budget, groceries crush pop standard of living, and ~11k/week in interest pins the construction queue — while two attempted overseas invasions (Taiwan, then Korea) reveal that the navy is not ready and the army is the wrong shape. The phase ends with Per Capita Taxation finally enacted after four setbacks, a dedicated landing-marine corps trained, Responsible Government locked in via *Emperor in Parliament*, and Emperor Komei's sudden death handing the throne to a Meiji-era Goshu modernizer.

## Setting the stage

Phase 3 delivered the constitutional pivot and Great Power recognition — loan interest dropped from 26% to ~7.5%, the Power Bloc was formed, and Industrialize Japan completed with time to spare. But the election-meddle backfire at the end of ep12 left scar tissue: Appointed Bureaucrats had been substituted in for Total Separation of Church and State mid-enactment to stop the bleeding, and the cabinet was still recovering from the illegitimate-government scare. The strategic plan coming into ep13 was to convert the recognition dividend into overseas expansion — specifically a Korea invasion that would seed the SE Asian protectorate arc that Phase 5 builds on.

The actual constraints were elsewhere. The lowest strata were below expected SoL on groceries, clothing, and furniture; paper sat at ~38k and iron at ~46k of state spending; weekly burn hit -21k with ~11k of that being interest. Naval transport capacity was 13 — nowhere near a sealift — and the army was still skirmishers and line infantry, which carry a -25% naval invasion efficiency penalty. Patch 1.13.1 had quietly changed literacy to decay toward education access, so literacy was sliding for non-obvious reasons. Per Capita Taxation had been on the queue for some time but kept stalling. The three episodes of this phase are about fixing all of that in parallel, mostly successfully, while burning two attempts to project force overseas before the underlying machinery was ready.

## Narrative

The phase opens on a cost-of-living crisis. The state's own books show paper and iron as the top two line items, and the player walks the government goods view rather than the market profitability view — a deliberate frame change. A flavour event ("Japanese become obsessed with paper") is refused on the spot because raising pop demand for a good the state is a net buyer of is just a tax on the treasury. Fertilizer plants are queued in the highest-grain states to bring food prices back to normal; one grocery industry per state goes out instead of one mega-factory because local-supply effects compound state-by-state. A disease outbreak and a brief illegitimate-government scare (the Shogun recounted into government to fix it) compound the cash bleed mid-episode, and pausing the state construction queue does *not* fix the deficit — the private construction sector is still consuming goods at market prices that imports keep expensive. The fix has to be supply expansion, not a build freeze.

Naval construction pivots. Sealift for the planned Korea invasion is the binding constraint, and Ships of the Line take too long per unit of carrying capacity. The player switches to frigates, then cruisers, with a ship-design template that drops speed to minimum (the fleet bottlenecks at 9.5 anyway) and reinvests the points into screening, hull, and carrying. Diplomacy moves once: a Dutch defensive pact with a food-tribute clause is refused given the ongoing food crisis, then a Prussian defensive pact + trade privileges + obligation is paid for and locked in — not because Prussia matters militarily, but because it is the only diplomatic lever available to grow overlapping interests toward an eventual power-bloc partnership. The yield is modest (~400 of the ~1000 needed for level-3 overlap), and the lesson logged is that distance to Europe makes Japan's trade routes and influence levers structurally weak.

Then the navy is tested, and it fails. Ep14 declares on Qing for Taiwan on the reasoning that "Qing has no big navy, so Taiwan must be undefended." Mid-war the player discovers that the target province is tagged South China with 270 garrisoned troops, *and* the navy's organization has collapsed because supply-ship delivery has stalled — 59 ships assigned, 62.7/week needed, only 6/week actually delivering, root cause unknown. The streamer posts to Discord and reloads the save. No diplomatic cost is paid, but the episode is burned on debugging. The lesson is taken in full: state-region ownership, garrison size, naval supply throughput, and strait adjacency all need pre-flight verification, and "weak navy" does not imply "weak coastal defence." In parallel, a radicals-led revolution is conceded via Census Suffrage — the single law change that satisfied both the Liberal and the Radical movement simultaneously, picked deliberately over Wealth Voting which only defused one. Hokkaido development opens via founding an urban centre in the Ainu lands, triggering *Taming the North*; the Hokkaido / North Sea Circuit option is picked over Hitakami-do because assimilation is permanent and the loyalty/romanticism modifiers from the other branch expire.

Ep15 puts the army into the right shape for the next attempt. Skirmish infantry comes online once the first ammunition production is hot, but skirmish still carries the -25% naval invasion penalty. The decision is made to raise a dedicated 20-unit landing-marine corps — same combat stats as skirmishers, no invasion penalty, 50% more goods upkeep — and to base it in states with local supply of the required goods to keep input costs down. An offensive-trait commander (10% offense) is picked despite an authoritarian ideology, because for a specialist role unit-combat traits beat politics. Per Capita Taxation finally enacts after four setbacks, gated on the law's peasant-share crossover point (~70%) being reached and on the cabinet composition; an IG-strength concession is paid to the Goshu rather than a one-time 24,000 money payment because IG strength compounds across future law attempts. *Emperor in Parliament* resolves into Responsible Government — absolutists swap for royalists, no civil war. Nitroglycerin unlocks and is rolled out to mines (50 → 80 explosives per level), accepting more paper and fertilizer consumption plus a mortality bump because the binding constraint was explosives supply for the military build.

Then Emperor Komei dies at 37. The sickly trait played out, the new Meiji emperor takes the throne, the modernizer ruler grants +25% prominence to the Goshu (lifting them to ~11% clout), and a previously-passable Appointed Bureaucrats attempt is suddenly contested again — succession reset the IG legitimacy landscape. Radical share is climbing toward 58% as the -32 suffrage modifier nears expiry. The phase ends with the *direction* finally correct (cleaner IGs, marine corps trained, income tax in, food prices stabilising, mines upgraded) but the immediate political ground shifting under the player's feet.

## Key decisions

- **Refuse "Japanese become obsessed with paper" event** — what: pick the "monks → +bureaucracy" branch instead of the cultural-obsession modifier. Alternatives: take the obsession buff for flavour reasons. Reasoning: paper is the state's #2 government good (~38k/week); raising pop demand for it raises the price the state itself pays. The bureaucracy boost is unambiguously cheaper. Outcome: paper consumption stable rather than spiking. [ep13](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep13.md)

- **Build paper and iron supply to crush their market price** — what: dedicate state construction to paper mills and iron mines, sized to push price toward "normal." Alternatives: chase market-profitable builds. Reasoning: government goods view ranks paper (~38k) and iron (~46k) as the two largest state expenditures; the saving on government spend dwarfs the building's own balance sheet. Outcome: phase ends with both prices closer to normal and government overhead lower. [ep13](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep13.md)

- **Switch naval construction from Ships of the Line to frigates, then cruisers** — what: re-queue the shipyards. Alternatives: keep building SoL (0.55 carrying each), or wait for the locked troop-ship tech. Reasoning: only 13 transport capacity and Korea invasion needs sealift; cheaper frigates (0.40 carrying) and cruisers reach capacity faster per construction-week than SoL. Frigate template drops speed to minimum (fleet bottlenecks at 9.5 anyway) and reinvests into screening, hull, carrying. Outcome: first cruiser launched mid-episode; sealift growing but still inadequate at episode close. [ep13](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep13.md)

- **Refuse Dutch defensive pact (with food-tribute clause), accept Prussian defensive pact + trade privileges + obligation** — what: pay the obligation to lock the Prussian pact at max duration. Alternatives: stay diplomatically idle; accept the Dutch terms. Reasoning: ongoing food crisis makes the Dutch tribute clause unaffordable; Prussia is the only diplomatic lever currently available to grow overlapping interests, trade privileges make Prussian-export goods cheaper to import. Outcome: relations maxed with Prussia, ~400 overlapping-interest gained — far short of the ~1000 needed for level-3 alliance-grade involvement. [ep13](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep13.md)

- **Stay on Per Capita Taxation through four enactment setbacks** — what: refuse to swap to an easier law despite repeated stalls. Alternatives: enact something else and reattempt later. Reasoning: roughly at the ~70% peasant threshold where per-capita starts outperforming land-based (land-based halves peasant tax and adds tax on all non-peasants; per-capita wins once non-peasant income share is large enough); fulfils a Goshu promise; introduces an actual income tax. Outcome: enacted in ep15 after the cabinet composition aligned; revenue restored, peasants relieved. [ep13](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep13.md), [ep15](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep15.md)

- **Concede the imminent revolution with Census Suffrage** — what: enact Census Suffrage rather than Wealth Voting. Alternatives: any law that defused only one of the two active movements. Reasoning: Radicals' revolution had progressed too far to stall; both Liberal and Radical movements listed Census Suffrage as a demand, so one law change defuses two movements. Outcome: both movements de-escalated in a single enactment. [ep14](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep14.md)

- **Save-scum and abort the Taiwan war** — what: reload after declaring on Qing. Alternatives: press the invasion. Reasoning: target province was tagged South China with 270 garrisoned defenders (not undefended as assumed), navy organization had collapsed from an unexplained supply-ship throughput failure (59 assigned, 62.7/week needed, 6/week actually delivering). Outcome: no diplomatic cost paid; the episode burned on diagnostics with the root naval supply bug unresolved. [ep14](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep14.md)

- **Pick Hokkaido / North Sea Circuit and settle via armed-forces colonists** — what: choose the assimilation + agricultural-bonus fork over Hitakami-do; pick armed-forces settler over peasant assimilation. Alternatives: loyalty + Romanticism IG boost; the peasant path. Reasoning: assimilation is a permanent demographic gain, loyalty/Romanticism modifiers expire; military IG was the IG most in need of loyalty so its journal reward had the most leverage. Outcome: Sapporo founded, *Taming the North* active, agricultural college built — gates met except for the 1M GDP threshold. [ep14](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep14.md)

- **Pre-build ammunition + explosives before retraining the army; raise a dedicated 20-unit landing-marine corps** — what: sequence two ammunition factories and an explosives factory online first, then build marines specifically (not just upgrade existing infantry). Alternatives: reuse skirmish/mobile/artillery/shrapnel infantry for the invasion. Reasoning: every non-mechanized infantry type carries -25% naval invasion efficiency; marines cost 1.5/2.5 goods vs 1/2 but have identical combat stats and no penalty. Base them in states with local supply of the required goods. Pick an offensive-trait commander (10% offense) despite authoritarian ideology because for a specialist invasion army, unit-combat traits beat politics. Outcome: 20-unit amphibious corps formed under an offensive commander; ammunition pipeline running. [ep14](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep14.md), [ep15](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep15.md)

- **Complete *Emperor in Parliament* into Responsible Government** — what: resolve the journal entry. Alternatives: leave it pending. Reasoning: replaces absolutists with royalists (a cleaner IG landscape, royalists prefer monarchy over traditionalism), avoids civil war. Outcome: Responsible Government in place; future law attempts face a friendlier opposition. [ep15](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep15.md)

- **Switch all mines to the Nitroglycerin production method on unlock** — what: roll out the new PM, iron mines first. Alternatives: keep current PMs to avoid the input/SoL costs. Reasoning: explosives 50 → 80 per level, more iron output; the binding constraint was explosives supply for the military build, and the paper/fertilizer/mortality costs were second-order. Outcome: explosives throughput up, mine output up, military prices easing. [ep15](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep15.md)

- **Accept IG-strength concession (not cash) to push Appointed Bureaucrats after Meiji succession** — what: grant IG strength to the favoured Goshu/modernizer faction instead of paying 24,000 money or accepting cultural exclusion. Alternatives: pay the cash, take the exclusion, or wait. Reasoning: IG-strength concessions to a favoured group compound across future law attempts; cash is one-shot. Pursue Appointed Bureaucrats rather than an assembly-rights-style radical-pleasing law because the binding constraint is government building taxation capacity, and it weakens aristocrats. Outcome: Appointed Bureaucrats remains contested at episode end (succession reset the IG legitimacy), but the concession is banked in the right group. [ep15](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep15.md)

## Phase-specific lessons

- **Audit government goods, not just market profitability.** The state's own purchase line items often dwarf any individual building's profit. Sort government goods spend, identify the top 1–2 items (paper, iron, small arms, services for Japan-shaped economies), and build dedicated state supply specifically to crush their *price* — not to chase margin. The corollary: decline any event that raises pop demand for a good the state is a net buyer of. The "obsession" modifier is a treasury tax in disguise.

- **Match naval construction to the bottleneck you actually have, not the doctrine you remember.** Ship-of-the-line dominance is correct for fleet engagements; it is the wrong answer when sealift per construction-week is the limit. Optimise carrying-capacity-per-build-time, not per-hull. In the ship designer, don't pay for speed above the fleet's slowest hull — points spent above the bottleneck are wasted.

- **Pre-flight checklist for novel invasions: state-region ownership, garrison count, naval supply throughput, strait adjacency.** The state your war goal sits in may be administered by a different tag (270 South China garrison guarding a "Taiwan" objective). Assigned supply-ship count is not delivered supply — verify throughput with a small test fleet before committing. "Weak navy" does not imply "weak coastal defence." Save before the declare button on any first-of-kind war so unexpected mechanics can be reverted without diplomatic cost.

- **Sequence input factories before retraining troops; raise dedicated marines rather than reusing line infantry for amphibious work.** Ammunition, small arms, explosives must be producible *before* the unit-type swap, or organization collapses on first engagement. Every non-mechanized infantry type carries -25% naval invasion efficiency — landing marines pay ~50% more goods upkeep for the same combat stats and zero penalty. Build them in states with local supply of the required goods, and pick commanders by trait fit (offense for invasions), not politics.

- **Pausing state construction does not fix a deficit caused by private construction.** The private construction sector still consumes goods at market price, and if those goods are expensive because of imports, the deficit persists even with an empty state queue. The real fix is supply expansion (build the input goods locally) or letting prices settle, not a build freeze. Once debt interest exceeds ~10% of weekly expenses, *new* construction becomes self-defeating regardless — pay down debt or raise taxes before resuming the queue.

- **Plan for legitimacy resets at succession.** A sudden ruler death can flip your cabinet composition, reset IG legitimacy, and contest laws that were previously passable. Expect to lose mid-enactment laws when the throne changes. Heir-IG matters precisely because a modernizer/Goshu heir compounds on succession (+25% prominence to their IG here), but the same mechanic cuts both ways — the new ruler can hand prominence to an IG that doesn't support your current law.

## What to take into Phase 5

Phase 4 has assembled the missing pieces — landing marines, Per Capita Taxation revenue, Responsible Government, food and government-good prices stabilising, a Meiji-era modernizer on the throne — but Appointed Bureaucrats is still contested and the navy hasn't actually proven itself in a war. Phase 5 cashes those pieces in via protectorate-first expansion against weak SE Asian neighbours, with the binding caveat that the player must stay diplomatically *free* during truce windows or a fresh subject will break loose under the "failed to protect" mechanic.
