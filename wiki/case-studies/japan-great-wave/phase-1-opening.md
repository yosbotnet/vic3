# Phase 1 — Opening the closed country

**Episodes:** [ep01](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep01.md) · [ep02](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep02.md) · [ep03](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep03.md)

**TL;DR:** Three episodes spent prying open a locked-shut Tokugawa Japan: surviving the Tempo Crisis timer and a country-wide famine, cheapening construction by ~30%, dragging Chonin clout up enough to matter, and then cashing in the Miroslavski incident to revoke Sakoku, swap to Mercantilism, and annex Ryukyu — all before the "Picking the Lock" launch window starts ticking.

## Setting the stage

Japan opens in 1836 as the Tokugawa Shogunate under Sakoku and Closed Borders. The market is sealed off, migration is blocked, and the cabinet is dominated by a single hostile interest group — the Daimyo — which makes almost every law swap a 0% or near-0% pass chance. The Edo Status caste system silently strangles qualifications across the whole economy even after the dev patch halved its penalty. Sitting on top of this: a 12-year Tempo Crisis timer, a country-wide famine event ("Era of Heavenly Protection") that fires on turn one, ~16.6M pops in mild starvation, and a Merchant Navy law whose only useful corner is +30% supply-ship construction efficiency. Construction sectors run wooden buildings because Japan does not yet make iron in any meaningful volume, and shipyards cannot produce military hulls because the cheapest viable ship-of-the-line wants artillery and iron the country simply does not have [ep01](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep01.md).

The standard "swap to a better trade law, click some decrees, recruit a tech tree" opening does not work here. Every lawful exit from Sakoku is blocked: no political movement yet exists that wants opening, so the lawful reform path has no support to ride; Japan is too weak to invite a great-power gunboat opening; and the scripted event has not fired. The cabinet cannot legislate the reformer-side journal goals because the only interest groups that want them — Chonin and Rangakusha — sit below the 5% clout threshold required to enter cabinet. The first dozen years cannot be about passing laws. They have to be about repositioning.

## Narrative

The first episode is a survey of what Japan can and cannot do, ending with the player committing to the reformer side of the Tempo Crisis rather than the hardliner side — a directional bet that the Chonin, not the Samurai, will be the engine of the run. Promote Social Mobility decrees are placed in every state to fight the Edo Status qualifications penalty; the rest of authority is held for consumption taxes on liquor and tobacco. The "combat rice hoarding" agenda fires the moment the grain-price threshold is met, removing the +10% peasant goods consumption modifier and giving the starving pop majority a small SoL bump. Naval Fortifications go down in Kanto and Osaka to chase the Edo–Osaka Confiscation Order objective. Shipyards run 100% on supply ships under the Merchant Navy law because military hulls are an unaffordable fantasy. A long-shot attempt to swap Local Police → Dedicated Police Force is started by bribing the Devout — the most collaborative opposing IG — with political concessions, lifting pass chance from effectively 0% to ~14% [ep01](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep01.md).

Episode two is the construction-cheapening run. Logging camps go up in resource-rich Kyushu and Shikoku — not in Kanto, because iron is the real bottleneck and Shikoku alone has 16 iron mines waiting on a production-method upgrade. A tooling workshop is co-located with the iron mine in Kanto to dodge the state-vs-market price gap that an isolated 65%-market-access economy can't otherwise close. Logging camps first, then the tooling workshop, then the iron mine, then the sawmill PM, then iron-tools PMs — and inside a single episode construction cost drops from ~32k to ~22k. The army is trimmed from 58 to 51 battalions, exactly the 500 Power Projection / 50-battalion threshold Tempo requires, to free upkeep that was burning for nothing. Dedicated Police Force passes on its second attempt after the player spends a setback to buy +10% pass chance, trading one character's popularity for a load-bearing law. National Guard is queued next, picked specifically because the only movement opposed (Sabaku peasants) sits below 25% activism and therefore generates no stall chance. A Chonin petition for Capital Fleets is accepted, a Peasant Movement petition is accepted, and privatization is set as the default posture — the player's stated long-term plan is an industrialized empire run by private capital, with the Emperor at the top [ep02](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep02.md).

Episode three is the unlock. The Miroslavski incident fires; the player takes the "review Sakoku" branch — the only one of the three options that actually unlocks the Revoke Sakoku button — and accepts a -67% Daimyo approval malus because it decays in 5 years inside an 80-year horizon. Sakoku is revoked immediately rather than stockpiled for a more prepared moment, because the "Picking the Lock" modifier that fires on revocation gives Chonin, Rangakusha, and industrialists +25% strength for only 10 years and the whole point is to overlap that window with the upcoming law-change queue. Mercantilism replaces the old trade law in the same click — the long-term plan is a protectionist empire, and the Daimyo are already angry. Three new journals open: Earning Recognition, the Hidden Domain, and the Honorable Restoration [ep03](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep03.md).

The Hidden Domain is resolved in the same episode. Qing has the naval projection lead in the Ryukyu sway contest, so brute infrastructure (a level-2 port or shipyard in Ryukyu) is rejected as too expensive, and the state-religion-gated Kiku Ogimi path is law-locked. Instead the player stacks three cheap diplomatic pacts — Military Access Treaty, a Money Transfer maxed for obligation points, and Mutual Obligation — to unlock the "inspect coastal defenses" interaction. The combined +5 monthly sway outpaces Qing, and Ryukyu is annexed inside the episode. The Rangakusha morph into the Intelligentsia via the Practical Learning event, and their new ideology (Migration Controls, Protectionism, Constitutionalist) happens to align with the player's plan. The Honorable Restoration path is not yet usable — Sonnō jōi oscillates around 0–1% — and a new traditionalist Shogun (Ieyoshi) takes power, locking out the parliamentary-reform button until he dies. The Petition for Imperial Restoration button is deferred because it requires Sonnō jōi at 50% and low Shogunate legitimacy produces "unforeseen consequences." Phase 1 ends with the country open, the market integrating, and a 10-year clock running on the only modifier that makes the next phase's reform queue plausible [ep03](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep03.md).

## Key decisions

- **Target the reformer side of the Tempo Crisis, not the hardliner side.** Hardliner buffs the Samurai and locks the country deeper into the laws Japan is trying to escape; reformer feeds the Chonin and Rangakusha — the only IGs that want the bureaucracy and Lex Status changes the long-game plan needs. Tempo itself was never seriously expected to complete, but the *direction* of every authority click, character promotion, and law-change attempt had to be set on day one. Choosing reformer made every later piece of work — Picking the Lock, Honorable Restoration, eventually Industrialize Japan — compound on the same Chonin clout investment [ep01](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep01.md).

- **Spend the entire first authority budget on Promote Social Mobility in every state instead of patronage or character promotion.** Alternative was the older-patch habit of patronizing/promoting characters to flip IG clout, but post-patch promoting low-strata characters now triggers approval penalties and the conversion rate is poor. Promote Social Mobility directly attacks the Edo Status qualifications penalty (which silently strangles hiring everywhere) and grants education access. Reasoning: with a near-zero legislation pipeline, the only useful authority sink is one that quietly improves every economic decision the country will make for years [ep01](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep01.md).

- **Slider shipyards to 100% supply ships and defer military hulls entirely.** Alternative was to try building any military hull early to start filling the Tempo Navy objective. Rejected because Japan's starting Merchant Navy law grants +30% supply-ship construction efficiency, the cheapest viable ship-of-the-line still needs 3 artillery + 5 artillery/3 iron for guns that Japan does not produce, and ship cost scales exponentially with each module. Supply ships are now the replacement for convoys under the new system and gate fleet endurance; mass-producing them costs almost nothing and is exactly what the active naval law subsidises. Military shipbuilding is sequenced behind Atmospheric Engine + a domestic artillery/iron base [ep01](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep01.md).

- **Build the wood-and-tools construction-cheapening chain in resource-rich states before anything ambitious.** Logging camps in Kyushu and Shikoku, then a tooling workshop *co-located with an iron mine in Kanto* (to dodge the state-vs-market price gap when market access tech is weak), then sawmill PM, then iron-tools PM. Alternative was to build straight into shipyards or rice farms while keeping construction expensive. Reasoning: a 30% drop in build cost compounds across every later project — every Naval Fortification, every Urban Centre, every shipyard built in phases 2-3 inherits the saving. The order is forced (tools need iron, so the iron mine must come before the iron-tools PM), and one Kyushu/Shikoku iron base is also positioning for eventual steel production [ep02](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep02.md).

- **Trim the army to the exact 50-battalion / 500 Power Projection threshold.** Fired 7 battalions down from 58. Alternative was to keep the larger army as insurance against the famine triggering unrest. Reasoning: Japan is locked inside Sakoku, can't attack anyone, and every battalion above the Tempo requirement is pure upkeep drain in a treasury that cannot afford it. Upkeep saved redirects to subsidising the ports, iron mine, and tooling workshop while they fail to hire at base wages [ep02](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep02.md).

- **Bribe the Devout with political concessions to drag Dedicated Police Force from 0% → 14% pass chance, then spend a setback for +10%.** Alternatives were to do nothing (guaranteed zero progress) or to chase a higher-value law at the same 0%. The Devout were chosen because they were the most collaborative of the opposing IGs and only cared about strength (so political concessions, not cash, was the right currency). Spending a setback on a non-critical character's popularity to buy +10% pass chance on a *load-bearing* law (it unlocks the next reform laws) succeeded — Police Force passed in episode 2, removing 5% from the law-enactment penalty stack [ep01](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep01.md), [ep02](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep02.md).

- **Refuse to promote Chonin into cabinet the moment they hit Influential.** Alternative — the default-tutorial move — was to take the IG-strength bump immediately. Rejected because promoting Chonin then would have flipped the powerful Daimyo from neutral to hostile, doubling stall chance on every subsequent enactment from 25% to 50%. Stall chance is multiplicative, and a neutral powerful IG is worth more than the strength gain from elevating a barely-eligible reformer. Chonin promotion was postponed until they could be added *alongside* the Daimyo without flipping them [ep02](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep02.md).

- **Pick National Guard as the next law specifically because the only opposing movement is below the 25% activism threshold.** Alternative was something more substantively impactful (a serfdom-adjacent law, a voting law). Rejected because most of those have opposition movements above 25%, which would have re-introduced stall chance. National Guard buffs the Chonin (path to 15–20% IG strength at higher tiers) and the Sabaku peasants who oppose it sit below 25% activism, so the enactment runs with no stall penalty. Reasoning: in a locked-cabinet phase, who opposes a law matters more than what it does — choose laws that can actually pass to keep the law slot moving [ep02](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep02.md).

- **Take the "review Sakoku" branch of the Miroslavski incident even at -67% Daimyo approval.** Alternatives were to back the garrison (only damages Prussia relations, no reform unlock) or apologise (angers Daimyo without unlocking anything). The "review" branch is the only one that unlocks the Revoke Sakoku button. The -67% Daimyo malus is double the -44% of the apology, but both decay in 5 years — and 5 years is nothing inside an 80-year horizon. Reasoning: compare malus duration to strategic horizon, not headline number [ep03](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep03.md).

- **Revoke Sakoku and swap to Mercantilism immediately rather than waiting for the National Guard institution and Chonin clout to grow first.** The cautious alternative was to delay until the country was more prepared. Rejected because "Picking the Lock" — the modifier that fires on revocation — gives Chonin / Rangakusha / industrialists +25% strength for only 10 years. It is a launch window, not a buff to hoard: every month of delay before clicking shrinks the overlap between the modifier and the law-change queue it is meant to power. Mercantilism is bundled in the same political beat because the additional Daimyo anger shares the 5-year decay clock with the revoke malus anyway [ep03](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep03.md).

- **Annex Ryukyu via stacked cheap diplomatic pacts, not infrastructure.** Alternatives considered and rejected: reaffirm suzerainty (Qing has the naval-projection lead in the sway contest), build a level-2 port or shipyard in Ryukyu (huge infrastructure commitment in a contested state), or take the Kiku Ogimi state-religion-gated path (law-locked). Chose Military Access Treaty + a Money Transfer maxed for obligation points + Mutual Obligation. Combined +5 monthly sway from "inspect coastal defenses" + the transfer beat Qing's lead; Ryukyu fell inside the same episode the path was opened [ep03](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep03.md).

- **Defer the Petition for Imperial Restoration button and route around the traditionalist Shogun rather than fight succession RNG.** The new Shogun Ieyoshi is traditionalist, and so is the heir, which locks out the parliamentary-reform-strategy button. The Restoration button itself requires Sonnō jōi at 50% — currently 0–1% — and low Shogunate legitimacy produces "unforeseen consequences." Reasoning: don't trigger high-risk movement events at low legitimacy without a fallback, and don't wait on succession RNG when an alternative slow path exists (kill the Bakufu through law and clout changes, then take the Emperor of Japan for six months, no civil war). This punts the Restoration to Phase 2 [ep03](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep03.md).

## Phase-specific lessons

- **A locked starting cabinet means the first 12-year window is repositioning, not legislating.** When one dominant IG can stall every law into the ground, the real first objective is invisible: lift a marginalized reformer IG above the 5% cabinet-eligibility threshold so any of the journal goals you can see actually become reachable. Spend authority on decrees and movement attraction, not on doomed law swaps.

- **Pick the next law by who opposes it, not by what it does.** Movement activism has to be above 25% to count as stall chance, so the highest-throughput laws in a stuck political landscape are ones whose opposing movements sit below 25%. Pair this with the multiplicative rule — don't promote a newly-eligible reformer into cabinet if it flips a neutral powerful IG to hostile, because doubling stall chance usually outweighs the IG-strength gain.

- **Time-limited "opening" modifiers are launch windows, not buffs to hoard.** A one-shot 10-year strength boost to your reform IGs (Picking the Lock) is worth the most when it overlaps the law-change queue, so fire it the moment you can rather than stockpiling preparation. The flipside: compare the *duration* of any negative modifier to your strategic horizon before flinching at the headline number — a -67% malus that decays in 5 years is cheaper than a permanent -44% blocker.

- **Construction-cheapening before everything else, sequenced and co-located.** Logging camps → tooling workshop → iron mine → enable sawmill and iron-tools PMs. The chain is forced (tools need iron) and the gain compounds across every later project. Co-locate inputs in one state when market access tech is weak — the state-vs-market price gap on isolated low-infrastructure economies can wipe out the apparent profit of the chain itself.

- **For sway contests, stack cheap diplomatic pacts instead of building infrastructure in the contested state.** Military Access Treaty + a maxed Money Transfer + Mutual Obligation can outscore a naval-projection-led contest more cheaply than a port or shipyard would, and the cash on transfer/obligation deals should be maxed because the deal-points formula rewards maximum transferable amount.

## What to take into Phase 2

End-of-phase state: Sakoku revoked, Mercantilism in force, Ryukyu annexed (Nansei Shoto incorporated, sugar plantations available), Dedicated Police Force passed, National Guard enacting with no stall chance, construction cost down ~30%, Rangakusha morphed into Intelligentsia and aligned with the player's plan. Live risks queued for Phase 2: the "Picking the Lock" +25% reform-IG modifier is already counting down its 10 years, the Tempo Crisis timer is still ticking with the reformer goals only partially reachable, the Honorable Restoration is open but Sonnō jōi sits near zero, a traditionalist Shogun blocks the parliamentary-reform path, the Daimyo are at -67% approval for the next 5 years, and the famine pressure on ~16.6M pops has not been structurally fixed.
