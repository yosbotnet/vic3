# Phase 3 — Recognition and the constitutional pivot

**Episodes:** [ep09](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep09.md) · [ep10](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep10.md) · [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md) · [ep12](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep12.md)

**TL;DR:** Four episodes spent crawling out of unrecognized-power debt: Landed Voting is enacted as a constitutional-monarchy detour to flip the cabinet, Lex Edo Status finally clears the Warrior Caste blocker, Professional Army retires the samurai, Austria's Treaty of Commerce and Navigation grants Recognized Power status (Dec 1859) and drops loan interest from 26% to ~7.5%, and the Industrialize Japan journal is cheese-completed with 1y6m to spare by spamming Government Administrations — capped by an election meddle that backfires into an illegitimate government.

## Setting the stage

Phase 2 ended with the Komei Restoration timer started and three 12-year journal entries open in parallel: Dispatch a Mission to the West, Industrialize Japan, Retire the Samurai. The board state was hostile to all three. The cabinet was Kazoku-dominated (the rebranded Daimyo), Warrior Caste blocked Professional Army, and Warrior Caste itself could not be touched while the Edo Status law remained Rigid/Loosened. Interest payments were climbing past 9k/month because Japan was still unrecognized — a 26% loan interest rate that made every construction wave a treasury crisis. The Tempo Crisis was already conceded, and the Sonno Joi movement was angry at every treaty Japan had signed.

The strategic question was: what unblocks what, and in what order? The phase's answer is an unusually intricate chain — bolster Sonno Joi to push Lex Edo Status onto the ballot, swap to constitutional monarchy via Landed Voting so the cabinet can be re-stacked, retire the samurai, grind embassies and a foreign-investment treaty until Austria offers recognition, then sprint the Industrialize Japan urbanization gate with the cheapest building in the game. The phase delivers all of it, then immediately burns the recovered legitimacy on an election meddle that flips an interest group into opposition and leaves Japan without a legitimate government.

## Narrative

Episode 9 opens with the Modernization Movement active and the treasury bleeding. The player runs the full triage hierarchy: pause construction, redirect all construction sectors to Iron Frame Buildings (one PM tier), trim wages, raise consumption taxes (eating 10 legitimacy with nobody actively protesting), sell government-owned buildings to private. The Shinto conversion arc starts — Smelt Temple Bells fires, religious schools come in, Church clout temporarily balloons in exchange for a long-term displacement path. A surprise: the Power Block formation tooltip no longer requires vassals, so "Empire of the Rising Sun" is prepared under Sovereign Empire — but activation is *deferred*, because the -60 relations hit would crater the very recognition grind the player is running. Agrarianism reaches Ascent after one defensive setback spent late, freeing investment-pool flow that stabilizes the budget. Background noise: an Austrian company opens a HQ in Osaka via foreign-investment rights and starts leaking dividends abroad; a Western-name event is taken to trade bureaucracy for influence/relations/authority; a Tokugawa exile bug leaves an army orphaned, worked around by merge-then-split under a new general.

Episode 10 starts to wire the political chain. The Taiko Proclamation lifts Shinto to ~20% and unlocks State Religion as a law option, but the immediate problem is structural: Warrior Caste cannot be swapped until Edo Status moves, and Edo Status cannot pass under a Kazoku-dominated cabinet. The intermediate move is Landed Voting — the landowners actually *want* it, passing it converts Japan to constitutional monarchy with parties, and the new cabinet can be packed with Chonin, Intelligentsia, Gosho, and Trade Unions. Sonno Joi is bolstered (not granted clout, because bolster is reversible and clout is not) to push Lex Edo Status onto the agenda. Russia's foreign-investment-rights treaty is cancelled to dissolve the anti-Russian league the samurai had joined. Political concessions to Lex Edo Status are accepted as effectively free, since the concession dissolves the moment the law passes. A samurai coup is suppressed mid-enactment, but the Lex Edo Status attempt itself stalls; the host plans to save-scum next episode.

Episode 11 is the recognition pivot. The save-scum experiment immediately discovers that stalled-enactment outcomes are *pre-determined per tick* — reloading and re-clicking the same in-game day produces the same roll. The only lever is changing the stall-chance modifier before the next monthly tick, via IG negotiation or bribes. Once that's internalized, the cabinet is reshaped to include Samurai and exclude only Kazoku — the precise composition that unblocks Professional Army first, then End Edo System. Irregulars are upgraded to Line Infantry (not Skirmish, because Skirmish needs Ammunition supply Japan doesn't have at scale yet), satisfying the "no peasant levies" Restoration condition. The Treaty of Commerce and Navigation with Austria lands on 4 December 1859: Japan becomes a Recognized Great Power, rank 7, and loan interest drops to ~7.5%. The Power Block is finally activated under the Vassalization principle. The Taiko Proclamation is reissued to adopt Shinto State Religion. And the player discovers the urbanization cheese — Government Administrations cost only 250 construction, build extremely fast, give +20 urbanization per level, and can be downgraded after the threshold pings.

Episode 12 is the journal-completion sprint and the failure. GAs are spammed across qualifying states; the Industrialized Japan urbanization gate (70% of states with urban centers at level 5) clears with 1y6m to spare, and the Korea claims + military-research-speed reward is taken. Securing the Power closes in one click by using "make person less prominent" on a powerful Kazoku character, netting 5 years of legitimacy. Mount Tambora analogue ("Eruption in Vestiyava") devastates Kanto and Chishima Retto, met with Emergency Relief decrees on those two states only — the rest is allowed to decay naturally. Then the meddle. The election fires; the player favors the Constitutional Reform Party; the meddled IG flips into opposition; the coalition collapses; Japan has no legitimate government. Total Separation of Church and State is mid-enactment, stalling and angering Trade Unions further. The recovery move is to cancel Total Separation and pivot to Appointed Bureaucrats — a law the surviving coalition can actually pass, which also directly fixes the chronic taxation-capacity problem. The episode also lays groundwork for Phase 4's economy story: fertilizer plants + sulfur mines + iron, Naval Administrations to crew the SoL fleet, and the SoL-only naval doctrine confirmed (frigates literally cannot damage SoL armor).

By the end of Phase 3, Japan is a Recognized Great Power with a constitutional monarchy, a Sovereign Empire power bloc, a Shinto state religion converging on Arahitogami, the Industrialized Japan and Securing the Power journals closed, and an illegitimate government recovering through Appointed Bureaucrats. The recognition discount on loans makes Phase 4's economy-under-stress work cheaper to fund, but the treasury is still bleeding from GA paper upkeep and the election misfire has cost mid-game political momentum.

## Key decisions

- **Form the Power Block but defer activation until after recognition** — Discovered the Sovereign Empire archetype no longer requires vassals once influence is sufficient. Formed "Empire of the Rising Sun" but *did not activate*, because activation imposes -60 relations on other GPs and the recognition grind is built on those exact relations. Alternative was to activate immediately for the leadership bonuses or to wait for vassals to exist. Outcome: activated cleanly later in [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md) under the Vassalization principle, with no recognition cost. [ep09](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep09.md), [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md)

- **Run the treasury-triage hierarchy before taking more loans** — Interest payments past 9k/month with 26% unrecognized rates. Sequence: pause construction → concentrate all construction sectors on Iron Frame Buildings (one PM tier) → trim government/military wages → raise consumption tax (10 legitimacy spent because nobody was protesting) → sell government-owned buildings to private. Loans were explicitly avoided as a compounding spiral. Outcome: stabilized to ~2k loss/month while still building. [ep09](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep09.md)

- **Adopt Western name conventions for the influence/relations trade** — Took the recognition-favorable side of the name event, trading bureaucracy (renewable via tech) for influence, authority, and relations (all currently scarce). Alternative: maintain tradition for cultural cohesion. Reasoning: the recognition grind is the bottleneck for everything; trade the renewable resource for the scarce one. [ep09](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep09.md)

- **Enact Landed Voting as an intermediate step before Lex Edo Status** — Direct attempts on Edo Status under a Kazoku-dominated cabinet were judged "almost impossible." Landed Voting is wanted by the landowners; passing it converts Japan to constitutional monarchy with parties, after which the cabinet can be re-stacked with Chonin / Intelligentsia / Gosho / Trade Unions. Alternative: straight-line attempts on Edo Status that would have stalled out the timer. Outcome: enabled the entire downstream chain (Lex Edo Status → Warrior Caste → Professional Army → End Edo System). [ep10](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep10.md)

- **Bolster Sonno Joi, do NOT grant clout or concessions** — Wanted faster Lex Edo Status enactment without permanently amplifying the IG that benefits. Alternative was clout grants or formal concessions, which are not reversible. Reasoning: bolster is reversible, clout is not. Accepted some radicalization as the cost of speed. [ep10](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep10.md)

- **Accept "political concessions to Lex Edo Status" as effectively free** — Of the negotiated concessions available, picked the one whose value collapses the moment Lex Edo Status passes (which is the law being negotiated). Alternatives — stable-crop quotas, local police — would have been binding and onerous. Reasoning: pick concessions that self-destruct with the enactment. [ep10](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep10.md)

- **Cancel the Russia foreign-investment-rights treaty to dissolve the samurai's interest league** — The samurai sat in the opposing anti-Russian league; killing the treaty removed the league, which removed the radicals. Cost: lost treaty benefits, worsened Russia relations. Reasoning: cheaper than fighting the IG directly. [ep10](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep10.md)

- **Stop reloading the stalled enactment once tick-determinism is confirmed** — Save-scummed the Lex Edo Status / Bureaucratic Imbroglio stall, discovered the outcome is locked at the prior tick. The only real lever is changing the stall-chance modifier *before* the next monthly tick — negotiate, bribe, accept a costly demand, then advance time. Alternative was to keep reloading or to spend 34k on the army-expansion bribe immediately. Outcome: accepted the army-expansion bribe to lower future stall chance, used a console workaround when still stuck (acknowledged), and absorbed the lesson into all later stalls. [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md)

- **Re-stack the cabinet around the next law, not the last** — For Professional Army and the End-Edo-System pair, the cabinet was reshaped to include the Samurai and exclude only the Kazoku — the precise composition that unblocks both. Alternative was keeping the Kazoku-led cabinet that had passed Landed Voting. Reasoning: cabinet composition is a lever per-law, not a default; pick the IG configuration whose exclusion set matches the next law's opposition. Outcome: Professional Army enacted, Edo System ended in the same composition. [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md)

- **Upgrade Irregulars to Line Infantry rather than skipping to Skirmish** — "No peasant levies" condition required upgrading. Line Infantry uses Small Arms (Japan already produces); Skirmish requires Ammunition (Japan does not produce at scale). Reasoning: pick unit upgrades by input chain, not paper stats. [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md)

- **Spam Government Administrations to clear the Industrialize Japan urbanization gate** — Needed urban centers at level 5 in 70% of qualifying states; organic industrial growth would have missed the 12-year window. GAs cost ~250 construction, build very fast, and give +20 urbanization per level — strictly cheaper per urbanization point than Universities or Art Academies (also +20 at 400 cost). Spammed across qualifying states, then sold most back down once the threshold pinged (paper upkeep was bleeding cash). Outcome: Industrialized Japan completed with 1y6m to spare. [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md), [ep12](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep12.md)

- **Adopt Shinto State Religion via the Taiko Proclamation, planning the unwind** — Adopting State Religion completed a Restoration journal entry and started the conversion threshold toward Arahitogami at 60% Shinto. Alternative: stay Total Separation. Reasoning: State Religion is a reversible tool — adopted to satisfy the journal, planned to revert once the threshold is crossed so the bonus persists without the wage/acceptance penalty. [ep10](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep10.md), [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md)

- **Close Securing the Power by targeting a Kazoku character with "make person less prominent"** — One-click completion via the character action rather than waiting for natural clout decay or adding the Kazoku to government (which would have failed the JE outright). Reward: 5 years of legitimacy, banked for the upcoming law work. [ep12](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep12.md)

- **Meddle for Constitutional Reform Party in the election (the misfire)** — Used state power to favor CRP, paying electoral confidence. Alternatives: favor Imperial Way / Imperial Rule, or do nothing. Reasoning at the time: wanted progressive law progression early. Outcome: BACKFIRED — the meddled IG was pushed into opposition by other coalition IGs, the government became illegitimate, and Total Separation of Church and State (mid-enactment) had to be cancelled to recover legitimacy. Recovery move: pivot to Appointed Bureaucrats, a law the surviving coalition could pass, which also fixed the taxation-capacity shortfall. [ep12](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep12.md)

## Phase-specific lessons

- **Stalled enactments are pre-determined per tick — change the modifier, not the click.** Reloading and re-clicking the same in-game day produces the same roll. The only lever is changing the stall-chance modifier *before* the next monthly tick: negotiate with an IG leader, sway, bolster a supporting movement, or accept a costly demand, then advance time. Tutorial framings of stalls as "random setbacks" miss this determinism; the practical implication is that save-scum-grinding the same tick is wasted time, while one well-targeted intervention before the tick is decisive. [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md)

- **Use an intermediate law to reshape clout when the target law is "almost impossible."** When the dominant cabinet IG blocks the law you want, look for a different law that (a) is wanted by that same IG and (b) shifts the political system in a way that lets you re-stack the cabinet. Landed Voting was the canonical example — landowners wanted it, it converted Japan to constitutional monarchy with parties, the new cabinet could be packed against the original blocker. The general pattern: don't fight the cabinet you have; find the law that changes which cabinets are even possible.

- **Pick concessions whose value collapses with the law you're enacting.** When IGs demand concessions to pass a law, prefer the concession that dissolves at enactment over concessions that linger. "Political concessions to Lex Edo Status" evaporates the moment Lex Edo Status passes; stable-crop quotas or local-police obligations bind for years. Read each concession against the law it's attached to.

- **Defer Power Block activation when relations are the bottleneck.** Forming a Power Block is one click; *activating* it imposes a -60 relations hit on other GPs. When the campaign's binding constraint is the recognition grind (which depends on those exact relations), formation should happen but activation must wait. The general lesson: prerequisites can change between patches, but action *costs* persist — re-check the tooltip and separate "can I do this" from "should I do this now."

- **Election meddling can flip an IG into opposition.** "Favor party" risks pushing the meddled IG itself out of the government coalition if other coalition IGs object — leaving you with no legitimate government and no path to enact laws. Don't meddle while mid-enactment of a controversial law; the stalled law will then bleed legitimacy further as the angry IG remains locked out. The recovery move is to cancel the stalled law, restore a workable coalition, and enact something the surviving coalition supports.

- **Recognition is the single largest cost lever in the early game.** Going from unrecognized to Recognized Great Power dropped loan interest from 26% to ~7.5% — every construction wave and military buildout becomes dramatically cheaper. Plan spending spikes for *right after* recognition lands, and treat the entire recognition grind (foreign investment rights as cheap diplomatic currency, embassy stacking, treaty-of-commerce targets) as the highest-ROI work on the board until it completes. [ep11](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/great-wave-japan/ep11.md)

## What to take into Phase 4

Japan exits Phase 3 a Recognized Great Power with cheap loans, a constitutional monarchy, three closed journals, and an illegitimate government in slow recovery via Appointed Bureaucrats — but the underlying economy is still bleeding on paper, iron, and food, with a fleet that has no sailors and a recently-cheesed GA stack chewing paper upkeep. Phase 4 is where state-spend auditing, fertilizer chains, and a first aborted naval invasion replace the political-engineering puzzle of recognition.
