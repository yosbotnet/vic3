# Cuba — Early & Mid Game, Step by Step

**Source:** [Trading in Sin as CUBA LIVE! | Victoria 3 GIGACHAD Live](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cn0RBR3aUfw) by Tarkusarkusar, 2025-12-10 (Great Wave DLC, early-access build). Notes: [01-early-mid-game](https://github.com/yosbotnet/vic3/blob/main/notes/cuba-trading-in-sin-live/01-early-mid-game.md).

This is the **click-by-click companion** to the [Cuba case study](cuba-spanish-restoration.md). That page is the strategic highlight reel; this one is the slow opening — a follow-along playbook for the **first ~2 hours** of a Cuba run, from the 1836 start through independence and your first protectorate war. After that the run opens up and you should **take it your own way** — the goal here is to get you a healthy, migration-pumped, sin-goods economy and out of Spanish hands, then hand you the controls.

!!! note "Patch context"
    From a December 2025 Great Wave DLC early-access stream, so a few specifics (the three Cuban prestige companies, the governor-request feature, some production methods) are DLC/patch-dependent — the *sequence and reasoning* transfer even if a number has since moved. Timestamps like `[0:13:30]` point into the source VOD.

## Phase 1 — The opening: infrastructure before plantations

The single most important opening idea: **you are playing a plantation economy, but you do not open with plantations.** Build the construction backbone first, or everything stalls on infrastructure.

1. **Before you unpause,** look at Cuba's three new prestige-goods companies — **sugar**, **cigars**, and **liquor**. The sugar one gives *migration attraction* and feeds the investment pool; these are what make a plantation-led economy actually work now `[0:12:31]`.
2. **Queue two Construction Sectors** — set one to *agriculture industries*, one to *resource industries* `[0:13:30]`. See [Construction](../economy/construction.md).
3. **Build the backbone, not plantations:** lumber camp → iron-tools workshop → iron mine. Swap tools to **steel tools** one week before the iron mine finishes (it makes tools far more profitable) `[0:14:31]` `[0:29:30]`.
4. **Railways are the binding constraint.** Cuba runs out of infrastructure fast; finish the iron→steel→motors→railway chain before you scale Construction Sectors. Drop the **Road Maintenance** decree as a stopgap `[0:19:01]` `[0:25:31]`.
5. **Decrees:** **Greener Grass Campaigns** immediately, for migration attraction `[0:15:00]`. See [Citizenship & Acceptance](../laws-and-politics/citizenship-and-acceptance.md).
6. **Research:** Stock Exchange first (a "no-brainer"), then Egalitarianism (Proportional Taxation + universities), then Joint-Stock Companies for company charters `[0:15:00]` `[0:33:30]`.
7. **Request a governor** (the new subject feature) and pick the **Influential Planter** (plantation throughput). **Reject the Experienced Extractor** despite the strong bonus — its 1% mortality is a hard no `[0:20:01]` `[0:21:00]`.
8. **Politics:** your Landowners are strong but happen to be **Reformers** — good. **Do not try to ban slavery yet** (landowner clout is too high to negotiate). Pass **Census Suffrage** — it's easy while the planters are happy `[0:15:31]` `[0:32:00]`. See [Passing Laws](../laws-and-politics/passing-laws.md).
9. **Public Healthcare** via the church-in-government institution, mainly for migration attraction (level 2, the +1 migration score, is gated behind Quinine) `[0:32:30]`.
10. **Diplomacy:** keep Spain friendly; declare interests toward **America** (trade) and **England**. **Do not join Spain's market** — there's no steel in it, so build your own chain `[0:18:02]` `[0:28:30]`.

## Phase 2 — The sin economy (cigars + sugar)

Once railways lift the infra cap, switch to iron construction, spam iron, and *then* go heavy on plantations.

1. **Found your prestige companies before foreigners buy the buildings.** Build tobacco plantations to **level 5 in at least two states**, found the **cigar company**, and grant it a **tobacco monopoly on investment rights** the instant it exists — otherwise foreign investors snap up your tobacco `[0:42:00]` `[0:45:30]`. See [Companies](../economy/companies.md).
2. Use the **Exploited Rollers** PM on cigars — more jobs means more migration attraction, which is worth more than the cheaper PM here `[0:42:31]`.
3. **Found the sugar company next** for its migration-attraction bonus; grant it a sugar monopoly `[0:47:00]`.
4. **Pump migration:** low taxes + Greener Grass + healthcare draw mass-migration waves (French, then Mexican — ~71k in a single event). Don't stop them. A cultural community has to exist in a state before pops migrate there freely, so ports + the decree + a little RNG seed new states `[0:38:30]` `[0:40:31]`.
5. **Trade:** build out trade centres (~44) to export cigars and sugar, and **stay in the Spanish market** for the European migrant flow. Once you have a navy, drop a **naval interest in North Germany** — Prussia is the biggest cigar buyer `[0:58:00]` `[1:06:01]`. See [Trade](../economy/trade.md).
6. **Reclaim your industry:** nationalise the French regional HQ (≈£450k of investments, then ≈£1M of coal mines) to stop dividends leaking abroad `[0:59:32]`.

## Phase 3 — Dominion without a war

You want diplomatic freedom but not the cost of an early war, and you want to keep the Spanish market. **Dominion** gives you both — and you can get it for free:

- **The conscript bluff:** add conscripts but **never raise them**. The inflated army strength is enough that Spain will grant **Dominion** for just an obligation. Dominion pays 25% tax vs a Colony's 30%, lets you run your own diplomacy, and keeps you in the Spanish market `[0:49:01]` `[0:52:00]`. See [Small-Nation Play](../diplomacy/small-nation-play.md).

## Phase 4 — The war of independence

1. **Build a navy** (basic + military shipyard) — you need it to project interest and to land in Haiti/Puerto Rico `[0:54:00]`.
2. **Pass the political package:** Universal Suffrage (negotiate Church support with political concessions, ~42% pass), then Proportional Taxation, then remove Migration Controls `[1:02:00]` `[1:08:01]`.
3. **Conquer Haiti under Nationalism** — Cuba's arable land runs out, and Haiti adds land, sugar, and dyes `[1:14:00]` `[1:21:00]`.
4. **Force the war:** expel Spanish diplomats to tank relations (so Spain won't grant independence peacefully), then declare with **Puerto Rico** added as a war goal `[1:23:02]`.
5. **Win it:** raise military wages, **drop artillery** (it's bad this early), and **defend Havana before you launch the Puerto Rico landing** — Spain will land in your capital if your army is away. Then cash in on Spain's **Carlist uprising** to land and capitulate them `[1:28:00]` `[1:29:00]` `[1:35:02]`. See [War & Naval Invasions](../military/war-and-naval-invasions.md).
6. **Payoff:** independence brings **immediate Great Power** status; the independence journal lets you **ban slavery**, unlocking Afro-Caribbean culture. Take **Parliamentary** government `[1:38:03]`.

## Phase 5 — Your first expansion (and the hand-off)

1. **Form the Caribbean Union power bloc** with the **Cultural Commonwealth** doctrine — it lets you grant Afro-Caribbean culture to Romance-speaking subjects, so they incorporate fast `[1:38:32]` `[1:41:31]`. See [Power Blocs](../diplomacy/power-blocs.md).
2. **Cheap protectorate wars** — New Granada, Venezuela, Bolivia — timed around great-power distraction (revolts elsewhere; wait for Brazil to leave a war first). Coastal capitals are easy naval-landing wins `[1:50:31]` `[2:06:01]`.
3. **Nationalise all foreign buildings (~£16M), *then* pass Laissez-Faire** — never the other way, and never switch to Laissez-Faire while you're still a subject, or foreigners buy your industry. Install a market-liberal agitator in the Petite Bourgeoisie and rig elections for the Liberals first `[2:10:02]` `[1:59:32]`.

**From here, play your own game.** You have a migration-fed sin-goods economy, independence, Great Power status, and a power bloc to grow. The back half of the VOD pushes deeper into South America and the late-game law tree — but the opening above is the part worth copying; the rest is yours to improvise.

## Pitfalls checklist

- [ ] Built the construction backbone (lumber/tools/iron/rail) *before* plantations `[0:13:30]`
- [ ] Took the Influential Planter governor, not the Experienced Extractor `[0:21:00]`
- [ ] Did **not** join Spain's market `[0:28:30]`
- [ ] Founded the cigar/sugar companies *before* foreigners bought the buildings `[0:44:31]`
- [ ] **Conscripted early** — the run's biggest self-named mistake was conscripting too late for the independence war `[1:29:31]`
- [ ] Defended Havana before launching naval invasions `[1:26:30]`
- [ ] Nationalised *before* Laissez-Faire, and never went Laissez-Faire while a subject `[2:10:02]`
