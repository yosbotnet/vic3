# Victoria 3 Strategy Wiki

A topic-organized strategy reference distilled from YouTube tutorials, focused on the **decision-rule** layer of the game: when to do what, why, and what to do when it fails.

## What this is, what it isn't

- **What this is:** A playable strategy reference. Every page is built around decision rules, rules of thumb, and playbooks pulled from tutorial transcripts. Pages cite the source video for every claim.
- **What this isn't:** A mechanics reference. For raw numbers, production tables, and game data, use the [official Paradox wiki](https://vic3.paradoxwikis.com/Victoria_3_Wiki). This wiki cross-links there throughout.

## Learning path (suggested order for a new player)

1. **[Standard of Living, Wages, and Wealth](pops/standard-of-living.md)** — the metric the rest of the game serves.
2. **[Construction](economy/construction.md)** — the single most important system; you spend most of the game managing the construction queue.
3. **[Building Balance Sheet](economy/building-balance-sheet.md)** — how to read what your buildings are actually doing.
4. **[Passing Laws](laws-and-politics/passing-laws.md)** — every long-term goal depends on changing laws.
5. **[Treasury & Deficit Spending](economy/treasury-and-deficit.md)** — once stable, debt is the lever for exponential growth.
6. **[Trade](economy/trade.md)** — sell your surpluses, source your shortages.
7. **[Companies](economy/companies.md)** — the late-game multiplier on building output.
8. **[Citizenship & Acceptance](laws-and-politics/citizenship-and-acceptance.md)** & **[Cultural Fervor](laws-and-politics/cultural-fervor.md)** — pop integration.
9. **[Political Movements](laws-and-politics/political-movements.md)** — how the people push back, and how to channel it.
10. **[Power Blocs](diplomacy/power-blocs.md)** — diplomatic infrastructure.
11. **[Army](military/army.md)** & **[Navy](military/navy.md)** & **[War & Naval Invasions](military/war-and-naval-invasions.md)** — military.

After the path, mix and match by need.
