# Choosing a Goal & Playstyles

## What it is

Victoria 3 has no built-in win condition. Unlike a 4X with a victory screen, it hands you a map and a simulation and expects *you* to decide what "winning" means — which is why new players bounce off it: they optimise locally with no idea what they're optimising *toward*. This page is the meta-layer above every mechanics page: pick the goal first, then let the goal dictate which systems matter. It distils the "What is your Goal?" and "War as a Macro Game" segments of Tarkusarkusar's 2025 comprehensive tutorial, where the framing is made explicit before an applied [Belgium run](../case-studies/belgium-conquer-colonize-react.md) puts it into practice.

The single load-bearing idea: **everything hinges on the economy.** In CK3 you can play a whole game and never marry, never fight, never scheme — you can skip entire mechanics. In Victoria 3 you cannot skip the economy. Every goal, including the political and the roleplay ones, routes through building jobs and generating demand first `[1:27:31][1:28:00]`. So the goal sets the *destination*, and the economy is always the *road*.

## The playstyle taxonomy

1. **GDP-maximisation** — the most common and most mechanical goal. GDP is just the total quantity of goods in your market multiplied by their prices, summed `[1:25:01]`. The archetype is a warless USA that declares almost nothing (maybe the Mexican-American War for California) and simply builds the economy as high as it will go. Decision rule: if your goal is the GDP number, war is a distraction — every battalion and every military good is GDP you chose not to build. See [Construction](../economy/construction.md), [Companies](../economy/companies.md), [Trade](../economy/trade.md).

2. **Standard-of-living maximisation** — make your pops as rich and happy as possible: good jobs, cheap consumed goods, low taxes `[1:25:30]`. Mechanically distinct from GDP-max (a high-GDP nation can have miserable pops, and vice versa). Decision rule: optimise pops for SoL and the loyalist/radical balance, and optimise the economy for GDP — they are *separate* objectives that happen to share the economy as a substrate. See [Standard of Living](../pops/standard-of-living.md).

3. **Ideological goals** — e.g. a communist Belgium. You pursue these by working *backward* from the target law: a Council Republic requires empowered Trade Unions, which requires the jobs that produce union-aligned pops, which requires the buildings, which requires the economy `[1:27:01][1:27:31]`. Decision rule: name the end-state law, then chain the order of operations back to a build order. See [Passing Laws](../laws-and-politics/passing-laws.md), [Political Movements](../laws-and-politics/political-movements.md).

4. **Roleplay / historical reenactment** — Leopold's Congo, "Abraham Lincoln, King of America," restoring a dead empire. Possible, but Victoria 3's characters are *far* less impactful than CK3's, so character-driven RP is indirect and the game won't reward it mechanically `[1:26:01][1:26:31]`. Decision rule: pick RP goals for the story, not the payoff, and accept that the economy still has to carry them.

5. **Challenge runs** — self-imposed constraints, e.g. "win without ever constructing a building." Often the constraint collides head-on with the economy thesis (a no-building run is probably impossible without conquering or annexing other nations' buildings) `[1:28:31][1:29:00]`. Decision rule: a good challenge run is one that fights the economy thesis rather than ignoring it.

6. **War as a macro goal, not a tactical one** — war is the tool for things diplomacy *cannot* buy, and it is priced in opportunity cost. Every pound of military goods is construction you didn't fund; every week a conscript spends on a front is a week it didn't spend earning profit in a factory `[1:23:00]`. Decision rule: "conquer the weak, because the cost to conquer them is nothing; make deals with the strong, so you never become the weak one" `[1:23:31]`. See [War & Naval Invasions](../military/war-and-naval-invasions.md), [Army](../military/army.md).

## Rules of thumb

- **You can't pursue a goal whose mechanics you don't understand.** "You can't attempt to become communist if you don't understand the politics; you can't attempt 10M GDP if you don't understand the economics" `[1:29:00][1:29:30]`. Learn the systems first, then the creative goals open up.
- **Pick the goal *before* you touch the map.** The applied run sets all three goals (conquer, colonise, reform) in the first minute, then every subsequent decision is measured against them `[1:30:00]`. A goal chosen after you've already built is a rationalisation, not a plan.
- **The goal decides which pages matter.** A GDP-max run barely touches the military pages; a reactionary-politics run lives in [Passing Laws](../laws-and-politics/passing-laws.md) and [Political Movements](../laws-and-politics/political-movements.md) and treats war as a quick means to an end.
- **Regressing your laws is the hardest goal class.** Forcing an *illiberal* end-state on a liberal country is, per the host, harder than winning the wars around it — because law direction is gated by character ideology, which is mostly gambling. Budget far more time and save-scumming for "go authoritarian" than for "conquer a neighbour." See the [Belgium case study](../case-studies/belgium-conquer-colonize-react.md).
- **Learn on a country that punishes mistakes.** Belgium is recommended as a teaching start precisely because failure is *loud* — a bad move gets you conquered or visibly stalls your economy, where on an easy country (Sweden, Switzerland, the USA) you can play wrong for decades and never find out `[2:25:30]`.

## How to use this with the rest of the wiki

Once you've named a goal, the [Home page learning path](../README.md) is the on-ramp to the systems it depends on. As a rough mapping:

- **GDP-max / SoL-max** → [Standard of Living](../pops/standard-of-living.md) → [Construction](../economy/construction.md) → [Building Balance Sheet](../economy/building-balance-sheet.md) → [Companies](../economy/companies.md) → [Trade](../economy/trade.md).
- **Ideological / political** → [Passing Laws](../laws-and-politics/passing-laws.md) → [Political Movements](../laws-and-politics/political-movements.md) → [Citizenship & Acceptance](../laws-and-politics/citizenship-and-acceptance.md).
- **Expansionist / colonial** → [Small-Nation Play](../diplomacy/small-nation-play.md) (early) → [Power Blocs](../diplomacy/power-blocs.md) → [Colonization](../diplomacy/colonization.md) → [War & Naval Invasions](../military/war-and-naval-invasions.md).
- **Any goal, always** → the economy. Start at [Construction](../economy/construction.md) regardless of where you're headed.
